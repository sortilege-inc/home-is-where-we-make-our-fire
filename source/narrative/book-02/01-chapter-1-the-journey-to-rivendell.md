# Chapter 1: The Journey to Rivendell

*Book 2*

*Cermië 4, 2965*

The gates of Moria had closed behind Celenneth, and she now found herself alone beneath the vast sky. The silence was almost disorienting after the constant echo of the dwarven voices and the oppressive stone walls. She adjusted the straps of her pack, feeling the weight of the mithril and the brazen head of Dero, and took her first steps into the open wilderness. Her journey to Rivendell had begun.

By midday, Celenneth had covered several miles, the air growing lighter with each step that took her farther from the darkness of Moria. She followed a narrow stream, its gentle babble a soothing companion. As evening fell, she found shelter beneath an overhang in a grove of birches. She made a small fire, keeping the flames low, just enough to stave off the chill that crept through the night. Sleep did not come easily—visions of Moria's shadowed depths haunted her, and the memory of Lady Elwin's cold, wraith-like stare lingered. But she knew she could not let fear or sorrow weaken her resolve; each day brought her closer to Rivendell, and that knowledge gave her strength.

The second day tested her endurance as she pressed into the rolling hills of Eregion. The landscape stretched before her—wild, vast, and empty. She kept her eyes sharp, skirting the edges of old ruins that stood as silent witnesses to an age long gone. By mid-afternoon, she reached the Loudwater River. Its rushing waters offered a chance to rest and refill her supplies, though her bow remained within reach, her wariness unrelenting. She crossed the river cautiously, each step over the slick stones demanding focus. The cold water bit through her boots, but she reached the opposite bank safely, her pace quickened by the thought of her destination.

Heavy clouds gathered overhead on the third morning, and the faint rumble of thunder echoed in the distance. Celenneth moved swiftly, her path carrying her through dense woodland. The scent of pine and damp earth filled the air, and her thoughts drifted to the task ahead—the gift she bore, and the message she carried for Balin.

Late in the afternoon, she emerged from the woods onto a ridge that overlooked the Fords of Bruinen. She descended carefully, the rushing of the river filling her ears. The ford had an ancient presence, as though the land itself held the memories of all who had passed this way. Beyond the ford, the hills leading to Rivendell rose, a comforting sight that urged her onward.

The final stretch of her journey brought her to the outskirts of Rivendell. Relief washed over her as she caught sight of the hidden valley, its golden halls glistening beneath the early light. Yet, as she approached, her steps slowed—a figure emerged from the shadows of the trees. An elf, bow in hand, his gaze as sharp as his drawn arrow.

"I come in peace," Celenneth spoke, her voice steady despite her exhaustion. "I bear a gift for Lord Elrond, sent by Balin of Moria."

The elf's gaze lingered on her for a long moment before he nodded. "Follow me," he said, introducing himself as Belegon. Though his voice was calm, Celenneth could feel his scrutiny as she followed him. Together, they made their way down into the valley, Rivendell unfolding before her, its beauty both serene and otherworldly.

Celenneth’s arrival in Rivendell was met with warmth. She was granted accommodations and told that Lord Elrond would see her the next day. That evening, she allowed herself to relax for the first time in what felt like an age. The comforts of Rivendell—the warmth of the hearth, the scent of flowers in the air, the soft glow of candlelight—brought a peace she had almost forgotten. For now, her journey was at rest, and she took solace in knowing that she had reached a haven, a place of wisdom and sanctuary.
