# Chapter 9: False Alarm

*Book 12*

The cabin was quiet but heavy with an unspoken tension as Celenneth paced the room, her hand resting on the growing curve of her belly. The fire in the hearth burned low, casting flickering shadows against the wooden walls, and the wind outside howled faintly, rattling the windows.

Celenneth winced as another sharp pang gripped her abdomen, her breath catching. She steadied herself against the edge of the table, closing her eyes and trying to focus on her breathing. The pains had started earlier in the evening, faint at first but growing steadily more intense, each one clawing at her composure.

From the bed, Linnea watched with furrowed brows, her dark eyes fixed on her partner’s every movement. She had been quiet at first, but as Celenneth paused again, her hand pressing firmly against her belly, Linnea sat up, her voice laced with concern.

“Cell, what’s going on?”

Celenneth exhaled sharply, trying to sound calm. “I think… I think the baby might be coming. But it’s too early, isn’t it?”

Linnea was beside her in an instant, her steady presence grounding. “Let me see, love. Come sit down.” She guided Celenneth to the edge of the bed, taking her hands gently as she knelt beside her. “The baby isn’t due yet, remember? This might just be your body preparing. But tell me—how does it feel?”

Celenneth shook her head, her frustration evident. “It’s strong, Linnea. And it keeps coming. I don’t know if this is… if this is it, but I’ve never felt anything like it.”

Linnea nodded, her own concern carefully hidden beneath a calm exterior. “Alright. Let’s take it slow. Breathe with me. We’ll watch it together, see how it progresses. If it’s time, we’ll be ready. And if it’s not… we’ll know soon enough.”

They sat together by the fire, Linnea’s arm around Celenneth’s shoulders, her presence a quiet reassurance. Minutes turned into hours as they waited, the pains sharp but irregular, slowly easing as the night wore on. Linnea didn’t leave Celenneth’s side, her soothing words and steady hands a balm to her partner’s frayed nerves.

By the time the first light of dawn crept through the windows, the pains had subsided entirely, leaving Celenneth exhausted but relieved. She leaned back into the bed, her breaths slow and even. Linnea, sitting close, brushed a strand of auburn hair from her face, her dark eyes soft with understanding.

“It wasn’t time yet,” Linnea said gently, her thumb brushing against Celenneth’s cheek. “But when it is, you’ll know. And you’ll be ready. We’ll do this together.”

Celenneth smiled faintly, her fingers curling over Linnea’s hand. “Thank you. For staying with me, for… everything. I was so scared.”

“There’s nothing to fear, love. Not when we face it together,” Linnea whispered, pressing a soft kiss to Celenneth’s forehead.
