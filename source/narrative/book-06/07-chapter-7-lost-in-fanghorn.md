# Chapter 7: Lost in Fangorn

*Súlimë 26–30, 2966*

The sun sank behind mist-cloaked hills as Celenneth stumbled into what she knew must be Fangorn Forest, each breath rasping, each step driven by stubborn will alone. Behind her the cries of the orc hunters thinned and lost themselves in the broken country she had barely escaped.

The forest loomed dark and ancient, its twisted branches clawing the sky. Fangorn was a place of shadowed legend — where paths shifted, trees kept secrets, and the unwary were never seen again. A cursed refuge. It was the only one she had.

The underbrush snagged at her cloak, but even worn to the bone her hunter's eye held. She read the ground for game-trails and shelter, and fashioned crude snares from root and vine along the narrow paths she'd marked. Hours later a snap brought her limping to a hare caught struggling in the loop; she dispatched it quick and clean, and the simple success of it steadied her — something ordinary, something she could master. Back at a makeshift camp beneath a vast old oak she skinned and roasted it, and for the first time since her capture, let herself hope.[^r-hare]

The next morning she went deeper after water, following the murmur of a stream, the ground treacherous with roots half-buried in moss. One caught her boot, and she pitched forward with a cry, her body twisting as she fell down a short rocky embankment into a cluster of boulders. Pain erupted white in her right leg, a sickening jolt through her whole body, and when she made herself look, her heart sank — the lower leg lay at an unnatural angle, broken.

For a long moment she only lay there staring up at the tangled canopy, unable to think past the pulsing agony. But despair was a luxury she could not afford. She dragged herself back against a weathered trunk, hauled herself upright, and searched with trembling hands for a splint — a fallen branch, long and sound; her belt, frayed but strong. Biting back a scream, she forced the bone into place and bound it fast. Fresh agony surged and left her gasping — but the leg was braced. Functional, barely. She collapsed against the tree, soaked in sweat.[^r-leg]

Darkness swallowed the forest again, cold and indifferent, and she huddled by the embankment with her makeshift spear across her lap, every muscle tensed for a threat she could not fight. Pain and exhaustion dragged her under at last, into a dream.

*She stands before the black gates of Dol Guldur, jagged spires piercing a sky of ash and fire. Cold whispers coil around her like serpents, promising power, demanding surrender. Through the choking mist come shapes twisted and inhuman, their soulless eyes gleaming, and they surge toward her — reaching, clawing—*

She woke with a strangled gasp, the still cold air of Fangorn sharp in her lungs, the pain in her leg a searing proof that she lived — and that escape had come at its own price.[^s-dol]

[^r-hare]: **Hunting** — first refuge in Fangorn; a snare takes a hare, a small mastery that steadies her after the escape.
[^r-leg]: **Wound / Journey mishap** — a fall down a rocky embankment breaks Celenneth's lower leg. She sets and splints it alone, in agony. A serious injury that will shape the months to come — and leave a lasting scar.
[^s-dol]: **Shadow** — a Dol Guldur nightmare, rendered in the signposted vision mode (present tense, italics); the fortress reaches for her even in sleep. The dark clings after the escape.
