# Chapter 7: Lost in Fanghorn

*Book 6*

*Súlimë 26-30, 2966*

The sun dipped low behind the mist-cloaked hills as Celenneth stumbled through what she realized must be Fangorn Forest. Her breath rasped in her chest; each step driven by stubborn will. Behind her, the distant cries of orcish hunters faded into the rugged terrain she had barely escaped.

The forest loomed dark and ancient, its twisted branches clawing at the sky like gnarled fingers. Fangorn was a place of shadowed legends—where paths twisted, trees whispered secrets, and the unwary vanished forever. It was a cursed refuge, but it was her only chance.

The thick underbrush resisted her passage, branches snagging her cloak like grasping hands. But even through her weariness, her hunter's instincts remained sharp. She scanned the unfamiliar terrain with practiced focus, noting potential game trails and sheltering trees. She fashioned crude snares from twisted roots and vine cords, setting them along narrow game paths she had tracked the evening before.

Hours passed in tense waiting. Then—a snap. Her heart quickened. Moving cautiously toward the sound, she found a snare pulled taut around the leg of a fat hare, struggling but alive.

Her fingers trembled with relief as she dispatched the animal quickly and cleanly. The simple act of a successful hunt steadied her—something normal, something she could control.

Back at her makeshift camp beneath a massive, ancient oak, she skinned and roasted the hare over a small fire, savoring its smoky, greasy warmth. For the first time since her escape, she allowed herself to hope.

The next morning, determined to find a water source, she ventured deeper into the forest, following the soft murmur of a distant stream. The ground beneath the towering trees was treacherous—hidden roots twisted like living snares.

As she navigated the uneven trail, her foot snagged on a root half-buried in moss. She pitched forward with a startled cry. Her body twisted as she fell down a short, rocky embankment, crashing hard against a cluster of boulders.

Blinding pain erupted in her right leg, sending a sickening jolt through her entire body. Gritting her teeth, she forced herself still, breathing hard through the agony. Slowly, she looked down—and her heart sank.

Her lower leg lay at an unnatural angle, twisted and broken.

For a long moment, she simply lay there, staring up at the tangled canopy of dark branches, unable to move or think past the relentless, pulsing pain.

But despair was a luxury she could not afford.

She dragged herself back toward the embankment, each movement a white-hot lance of agony. Clawing at moss and dirt, she hauled herself upright against a weathered tree trunk. Trembling hands searched for something—anything—to use as a splint.

A fallen branch, long and sturdy. Her belt, frayed but strong.

Biting back a scream, she forced the shattered bone into place, securing it tightly with the belt. White-hot agony surged, leaving her gasping—but the leg was braced. Functional. Barely.

Shivering and soaked with sweat, she collapsed against the tree, breath ragged.

Darkness enveloped the forest once again, cold and indifferent. Celenneth huddled near the embankment where she had fallen, her makeshift spear resting across her lap, every muscle tensed for survival.

Pain and exhaustion dragged her into uneasy sleep—and into *dreams.*

She stood before the black gates of Dol Guldur, jagged spires piercing a sky of ash and fire. Cold whispers coiled like serpents around her, promising power, demanding submission.

Through the choking mists, she saw dark shapes, twisted and inhuman, soulless eyes gleaming with malice. The shadows surged toward her—reaching, clawing—

She woke with a strangled gasp, the cold, still air of Fangorn sharp in her lungs. The pain in her leg was a searing reminder that she lived—and that escape came with its own cost.
