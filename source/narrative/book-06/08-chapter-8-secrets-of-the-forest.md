# Chapter 8: Secrets of the Forest

*Víressë 1, 2966*

The cold morning gnawed at her as she woke from restless sleep, the broken leg throbbing like fire. She flexed her fingers against the chill and made herself focus. The pale light through the dense canopy brought little comfort; Fangorn's shadows lay as deep by day as by night.

She could not stay by the embankment — exposed, weak, hunted. She needed better shelter, water, and more snares before hunger finished what the fall had begun. Leaning on a crude staff, she limped deeper into the ancient wood, her eye out for defensible ground, every sound amplified in the stillness: the far rustle of leaves, the creak of wind-weary branches, the calls of unseen birds. Once, resting against a mossy rock, a prickle ran up the back of her neck — the sense of watching eyes. She turned, weapon up, and found only silent forest. But the quiet had a wrongness to it, as though the wood were holding its breath.

She pressed on until she found a rocky alcove where twisted roots formed a partial shelter beneath an old ash, and sank down there, panting, and made a thin infusion of her last dried herbs to dull the pain. It was then a sound reached her — a soft, rhythmic tapping, like hollow drumbeats on bark, slow and deliberate, too heavy for a man's tread and far too purposeful for wind. The shadows shifted at the clearing's edge; for a moment she thought she saw a massive shape slip between the trees, near indistinguishable from the bark and foliage. Then it was gone, and the tapping ceased.

Whatever it was, it was watching, and it was in no hurry. She reinforced her hollow with shaking hands and kept her spear close, and the eerie silence lingered long after the sound had died.

When she could bear the stillness no longer she forced herself up and went toward the murmur of the stream she'd heard, weaving through moss-covered roots and towering trunks, and found it at last running clear over pale stones. She knelt awkwardly and drank deep, and in the rippling water saw her own reflection — worn, hollow-eyed, grim — and, just beyond her shoulder, a hulking mass barely told apart from the bark-covered trees. She spun, staff raised. Nothing. Only stillness, and wind in the canopy.

She did not relax. Whatever moved through this wood was clever — cleverer than any beast. She set makeshift alarms about her streamside camp, sticks wedged in stones, thin trip-lines of vine strung to brittle branches. And as she worked, her fingers brushed something that did not belong: cold metal, half-buried in the earth. She unearthed a rusted dagger, its blade scarred and pitted, faint runes etched along the dark steel — symbols she did not know, and yet that stirred something dark at the back of her memory. Holding it sent an uncomfortable thrumming up her hand, as though the blade remembered a purpose she did not. She should have let it lie. She kept it.[^t-dagger]

A low creak echoed from deeper in the woods then — a sound of straining wood under great pressure, unnatural and deliberate, and it came again, closer. The forest itself seemed to breathe. She backed toward her shelter, her broken leg screaming, and her foot struck something soft: tracks, pressed deep, their edges jagged like shattered wood, made by nothing she knew — something that walked, and was not flesh. The silence closed thick around her again. Whatever watched her was drawing near, and it was no orc, and no animal.

[^t-dagger]: **Cursed item** — Celenneth unearths a rune-etched dagger in Fangorn; it thrums with an old malevolence and stirs dark memory. She keeps it against her better judgment. (Its nature and any connection to later cursed objects is left for the continuity review, per the story bible.)
