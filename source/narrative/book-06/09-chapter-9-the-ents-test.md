# Chapter 9: The Ent’s Test

*Book 6*

*Víressë 1, 2966*

Celenneth crouched low, her back pressed against the massive trunk of an ancient oak. Her breath came in shallow gasps as she scanned the twisted expanse of Fangorn, searching for movement beyond the thick haze of shadows. Her staff rested across her knees, while the strange, rune-etched dagger lay secured at her side.

The deep, groaning sound came again—a resonant creak, like aged wood under strain. This time it seemed to encircle her, moving with slow, deliberate purpose.

*It knows I’m here.*

She strained her ears, desperate to track its direction, but the forest was a labyrinth of sound. Twisting branches crackled faintly, leaves shivered, and distant echoes distorted the source.

Suddenly—*snap.*

Her trip-line trap sprang with a sharp crack of brittle wood. *Something* had triggered it. She gripped her staff and rose, favoring her good leg. Every nerve in her body thrummed with anticipation.

The undergrowth rustled, and a shadow shifted between the trees—not hurried or panicked, but measured and intentional.

Celenneth held her ground. “Show yourself!” she commanded, her voice steady despite her pounding heart.

Silence fell, thick and oppressive.

Then, with agonizing slowness, a colossal form emerged from the dense foliage. Its skin was gnarled bark, ancient and scarred. Thick roots twisted down its arms like veins, and its massive legs cracked the earth with each ponderous step. Its head was crowned with jagged, leafless branches that spread like twisted antlers.

Two deep-set *golden* eyes glimmered beneath its bark-like brow—*watchful, old, and knowing.*

Celenneth’s breath hitched. This was no orcish horror. No twisted servant of Dol Guldur.

*An Ent.*

Its massive head tilted with slow, deliberate thought as it studied her—its gaze unreadable, weighing her presence in *its* forest.

For a moment, they stood locked in stillness, ancient and mortal staring across the thin veil between myth and reality.

The Ent finally spoke, its voice a deep, resonant rumble like the groaning of old trees in a fierce wind:

**“Hoooom… a *****wanderer*****... far from where she belongs.”**

Its words resonated through the clearing, measured and timeless.

Celenneth lowered her staff, her voice rough with exhaustion but steady. “I... seek no harm. Only shelter.”

The Ent’s golden eyes gleamed thoughtfully, as if considering something far beyond her simple plea. Its immense limbs shifted slowly, roots pulling free from the earth with a deep, echoing *crack.*

**“Shelter... is not freely *****given***** in Fangorn.”**

Its gaze lingered on her broken leg, the worn scars of chains on her wrists, and the dark, cursed blade strapped to her belt.

**“But... perhaps... you are not as lost... as you think.”**

The ground trembled faintly as the Ent took a slow, deliberate step forward.

**“Come... the *****forest***** will decide... whether you remain.”**

With no other choice, Celenneth steadied herself on her makeshift crutch and stepped forward, leaving the ruined traps and strange tracks behind.

The ancient trees shifted around her, branches creaking like old bones as the forest’s living will closed in. She was no longer merely *running* from Dol Guldur—she was *claimed* by Fangorn now.

The forest seemed to hold its breath as Celenneth followed the towering Ent deeper into Fangorn’s ancient heart. Its massive strides cracked roots and scattered leaves, but its steps were slow and deliberate, each movement resonating like the shifting of continents.

She struggled to keep pace, her injured leg throbbing with each uneven step. The tangled ground rose and fell in unnatural ridges, making her crude splint a torment to endure. Yet she pressed on, grimly determined. *Pain means I’m alive.*

The forest around them darkened as the canopy thickened, casting Fangorn in perpetual twilight. Ancient trees stood like solemn sentinels, their bark knotted with the weight of forgotten ages. The air smelled of damp earth, moss, and something older—*powerful.*

Without warning, the Ent stopped. Celenneth staggered to a halt behind it, bracing herself against a twisted trunk.

The Ent’s golden eyes glowed faintly as it turned its head with glacial slowness.

**“You tread... on sacred earth.”** Its voice rumbled like stone grinding against stone. “Few of your kind... *leave.*”

Celenneth tightened her grip on her makeshift staff but kept her gaze steady. “You led me here. I do not believe it was in order to destroy me.”

The Ent regarded her with long, unsettling silence, its thoughts drifting like autumn leaves caught in an unfathomable wind.

Then—**movement.**

The trees behind her *shifted,* their branches curling subtly inward like claws. Moss writhed over exposed roots, *breathing.*

The realization struck her like a blade—*the forest itself is watching.*

A deep groan echoed through the grove, vibrating through the soil beneath her boots. Roots snapped like coiled whips, carving shallow trenches around her feet. The ancient trees seemed to twist toward her, groaning with slow, predatory intent.

The Ent raised one massive arm, its bark-covered hand brushing the low-hanging branches above.

**“You bear... *****marks...*****”** It rumbled, its gaze lingering on the dark scars across her wrists—remnants of Dol Guldur’s chains.

*Accusing.*

“I am *no servant* of Dol Guldur,” Celenneth spat, fire sparking in her voice despite her exhaustion. “I *escaped* them.”

The ancient being studied her fiercely, unmoved by mortal emotion.

**“Escape... does not cleanse... what *****clings.*****”**

The cursed dagger at her side pulsed faintly, cold against her hip. She gritted her teeth, knowing she could not explain its presence.

Branches groaned louder—*judging.*

Her mind spun with fractured memories from her dreams—*chains dragging across stone… dark halls echoing with hollow commands… twisted figures offering power in exchange for soul-bound servitude.*

She *had* escaped, but Fangorn did not *trust*—*could not*—until it was sure.

Suddenly, the ground *heaved.* Roots snapped upward like striking serpents, encircling her boots and dragging her to her knees. Her staff clattered uselessly to the earth.

Pain flared in her broken leg, but she barely felt it over the crushing pressure of the forest’s ancient will weighing down her very soul.

Her vision darkened—*judged.*

“*You carry… darkness…*” the Ent intoned, slow and terrible. “*You will face it... or be consumed.*”

The world twisted, fading into *shadow.*

Celenneth found herself in Dol Guldur’s haunted halls—stone walls slick with decay, echoes of cruel laughter reverberating off cold iron gates.

Chains snaked around her wrists again—*unbreakable.* In the distance, towering figures emerged from the void, clad in flowing black, their hollow eyes burning with malice—*waiting.*

A familiar, insidious voice hissed through the darkness:

*"Come… you belong here…”*

Celenneth *fought.*

She ripped the spectral chains from her wrists with a desperate scream, raw fury igniting within her. Her pain, her fear—*none of it would control her.*

**“I *****escaped!*****”** she roared, shattering the illusion through sheer force of will.

Her breath hitched as she was *pulled* back into the living world, knees pressed into damp earth, the forest still wrapped around her. Her body ached, her head swam, her leg throbbed*.*

The Ent watched her in heavy silence, its expression unreadable. Slowly, the creeping roots loosened and withdrew, vanishing into the soil.

The trees’ groaning ceased. Fangorn exhaled.

The Ent inclined its massive head ever so slightly, as though acknowledging something unseen.

**“You… *****fight.*****”**

Its voice softened—just enough to hint at approval.

**“Perhaps... there is hope... yet.”**

The ancient grove fell still once more, leaving Celenneth alone in the silence of a forest that had *tested* her... and *allowed* her to remain—for now.
