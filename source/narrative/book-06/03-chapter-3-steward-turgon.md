# Chapter 3: Steward Turgon

*Súlimë 9, 2966*

The great hall stretched before her, its vaulted ceiling arching like the ribs of some vast beast, sunlight pouring through high windows onto pale stone. None of the grandeur eased the knot in Celenneth's shoulders as her boots echoed toward the dais, where the Steward of Gondor sat beneath the banner of the White Tree, his face grave, his eyes sharp as they took her measure.

She stopped a respectful distance off and inclined her head. "My lord Steward. I come as a ranger of the Dúnedain, bearing tidings of unrest in the north and east. I ask your time and your counsel, for the shadows lengthen, and Gondor's allies cannot stand alone."

He leaned forward, resting his hands on the arms of his chair, his lined face wary and curious both. "You have come far to stand in this hall, Celenneth of the Dúnedain. Speak plainly. Tell us why Gondor should turn its eyes north when its borders are pressed on every side."

"Because Dol Guldur stirs again, my lord. The orcs of the mountains grow bold, raiding farther and more often than in living memory. The Woodland Realm is beset; Dale and Esgaroth whisper of dark powers moving beyond the peaks. If Gondor does not strengthen its bonds with these realms, the Shadow will grow unchecked, and spread like rot into every corner of Middle-earth."

A murmur ran through the small council behind him. He raised a hand to still it. "The plight of our northern kin is not unknown to us. But Gondor's strength is not what once it was. Our forces are stretched thin against the east — the Easterlings are relentless, the corsairs of Umbar harry our coasts. I do not deny the wisdom in your words. Neither can I ignore what we face."

Celenneth stepped closer, her voice softening without losing its edge. "I understand Gondor's burdens, my lord. But strength does not come from standing apart — it comes from unity. The north remembers Gondor's aid in years past. Now the north asks the same in return. If the Shadow takes us one by one, no fortress, no city, no kingdom will be left standing."

For a moment the weight of his office seemed to press down on him. "Your words carry the fire of conviction," he said at last. "And yet the choice is not a simple one. Gondor cannot march north while its own borders crumble. We are not blind to the rising dark. But our resources are finite, and our enemies many."

A heavy silence fell. Celenneth bowed her head to hide her disappointment. "I have done my duty in bringing this to Gondor," she said quietly. "I can ask no more than that it be weighed."

"Weighed it shall be." His expression gave nothing away. "You have my leave to remain in Minas Tirith. I trust the city's hospitality will ease your road."

She left the hall with her footsteps ringing in the stillness, her mind churning. She had known the odds were slim — but to hear Gondor's hesitation laid bare stung worse than she cared to admit, and the weight of her errand sat heavier on her as she stepped out into a sunlight the gleaming towers did nothing to warm.[^r-steward]

[^r-steward]: **Courtesy / Undertaking** — audience with **Steward Turgon** of Gondor.
