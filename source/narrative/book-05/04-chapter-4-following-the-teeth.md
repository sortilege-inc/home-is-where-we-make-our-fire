# Chapter 4: Following the Teeth

*Narquelië 26, 2965*

Dawn brought a bitter wind off the plains, an edge that cut through the thickest cloak. They moved swiftly against it, Celenneth at the front reading the horizon, Ellarien at the rear. At midday they paused at a shallow stream to water the horses and check their stores — and it was then that Ellarien pulled open one of Mareth's satchels and held up an empty sack.

"Gone," she said flatly. "The seam's torn. The marsh, most likely — we've lost most of the rations to it."

Celenneth crouched to the other bags and felt the truth of it settle cold: a handful of dried meat and hard biscuit, barely a day's food between the three of them.

Linnea let out a breath but her jaw set. "We're not turning back. We'll make do."

Celenneth was already slinging her bow across her shoulder. "I'll find game. Settle the horses and wait." She went off into the tall grass, silent and quick, and it was not long before a rabbit broke cover; she nocked, tracked, loosed, and it dropped clean, and she murmured her thanks to the land as she took it up. Back at the stream Linnea knelt over a cluster of smooth brown mushrooms, glancing to Ellarien. "Are these safe?"

Ellarien crouched to look. "Fieldcaps. Common enough. They'll keep you alive, if not delight you." By the time Celenneth returned with the rabbit the fire was going, and they made a thin soup of the mushrooms and the last of the stores, the roasted rabbit turned over the flames. It was a lean meal, but it held. "We'll be fine," Celenneth told Linnea, who sat close and weary. "This is the one thing I do know."[^r-forage]

⁂

*Narquelië 27, 2965*

The next day carried them easily over smooth ground, even Linnea managing the reins one-handed with growing confidence. Shortly after midday a rise revealed a strange formation ahead — a jagged cluster of black rock jutting from the flat plain, weathered and cracked by centuries of wind. Celenneth drew rein, studying it. "I've seen that before. But I can't place it — north of the Riddermark, or south."

Ellarien came up beside her. "The Stone Teeth. We're south of them, which puts the Riddermark ahead — eight days' riding, maybe less, south and a little west." She nodded at the landmark. "Travelers steer by the Teeth the way sailors steer by stars. We're on the right road."

The tension went out of Celenneth's shoulders. "Then we have our bearings." They pressed on, the Teeth falling away behind them, the grass growing shorter and the soil harder underfoot — the land itself promising the Mark drew nearer.

⁂

*Narquelië 28, 2965*

They rode on under a sky that smelled of distant rain, and made camp at dusk in the lee of a cluster of boulders. The fire warmed their tired faces, and Linnea leaned into Celenneth's side, her good arm draped across her waist — and then wrinkled her nose and drew back.

"Ugh. Is that us?"

Celenneth arched a brow. "What do you mean?"

Linnea sniffed pointedly. "I mean we smell like a barn a month past its last clean straw. Between the riding and the smoke, we're ripe."

Celenneth laughed, the tiredness giving way to it. "You're not wrong. But unless you're hiding a hot spring out here, we'll have to bear it a while yet."

From across the fire Ellarien glanced up from cleaning her blade. "There's a stream half a day south," she offered, dry. "Bear yourselves till then, and you might smell of grass and flowers again."

Linnea grinned and nudged Celenneth. "You hear that? Grass and flowers. That's the goal."

Celenneth shook her head, the smile lingering. "Fine. And if you behave, I might even change that splint for you."

[^r-forage]: **Journey / Hunting** — with most of the party's rations lost to the marsh, Celenneth takes game and Linnea forages (fieldcaps) to keep the company fed across the open plains. Living off the land; provisions restored to a lean sufficiency.
