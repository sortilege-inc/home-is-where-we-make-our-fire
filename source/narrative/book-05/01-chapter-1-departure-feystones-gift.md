# Chapter 1: Departure / Feystone's Gift

*Narquelië 19, 2965*

They left the woodland halls at first light, with the forest still under its dawn haze. Eryndor and Arannis walked them to the edge of it, their farewells quiet and warm; Arannis set her hands on Celenneth's shoulders and held her gaze. "The forest keeps you in its care, even as we let you go." Eryndor clasped Linnea's hands in both of his, his amber eyes bright with fondness and a little grief. "May your road run clear and your arrows fly true. We'll meet again when the winds bring you."

Thalion himself came leading a sleek chestnut mare. "Linnea. This is Mareth. She'll carry you swift and sure — and when your road passes beyond the forest, she'll come home to us with my scouts." Linnea brushed the mare's soft muzzle and murmured her thanks, and mounted; Celenneth swung up onto Nimroch, and the scouts fell in ahead.

They were two — Ellarien, lithe, her deep chestnut hair bound back, her armor woven in the muted greens and browns of Elvish craft so that she all but vanished among the trunks; and Thalanar, tall and rangy, his midnight hair in a loose braid over a young face gone prematurely weathered, a dark grey cloak at his shoulders worked with silver in the pattern of running water. The four of them found a rhythm quickly, and the scouts' quiet competence was a comfort on a road that gave few.

They had gone some way when Celenneth reined in. "Is it true there's a great hart in these woods?"

The scouts traded a glance, and Ellarien answered, almost reverent. "It is. Antlers wide as a king's crown. None have taken it."

Linnea grinned and nudged her. "A challenge?"

"A challenge," Celenneth agreed, already sliding from the saddle. She crouched to the tracks, read them, and rose sure of the line. For hours she followed it — the trail thinning and vanishing and surfacing again under her eye — until they came on it at last in a sunlit glade: a great hart at graze, its antlers hung with moss and small flowers, as though the wood itself had crowned it. She signed for silence, drew, breathed, and loosed. The arrow flew true. The hart faltered and fell, its death clean and quick.[^r-hart]

The scouts knelt beside it with words of respect. Celenneth laid a hand to its coat. "A worthy quarry." The meat they shared among them for the days ahead; the great rack of antlers they gave to Thalanar, a gift to carry back to the woodland.

Night drew the wood quiet, and by the fire Ellarien produced a worn leather pouch and a carved pipe, its bowl stained dark with years. "Time you saw how the Elves mark a good hunt," she said, tapping in a pinch of something fragrant — earthy, with a sharp citrus edge that cut through the smell of roasted meat.

"Pipeweed?" Linnea asked.

Ellarien laughed, rough and easy. "Not your Dale leaf. This is feystone's gift. Breathe it deep, and it takes you places you've never walked." She lit it, drew, and passed it round. Thalanar took a pull and exhaled with a grunt, his sharp features easing. "Not for the faint-hearted," he warned, handing it to Linnea.

Linnea drew tentatively, coughing, then found the rhythm of it, and the world tilted bright around her. The fire leapt in impossible shapes and she laughed, giddy. "The flames — they're *alive.*"

"Good, isn't it," Ellarien said, and offered the pipe to Celenneth. "Watch your step, though. The visions can be kind, or they can be cruel."

Celenneth drew deep. At first it was warmth: the trees seemed to hum, their branches weaving patterns that hinted at some language just past knowing, and stars bloomed in the dark overhead like flowers. Then the warmth turned.

*The stars go out. The dark comes swirling in to fill the space they leave, and the laughter she has been hearing curdles into screams. She is on her feet and no longer by the fire. The trees loom hostile, their gnarled limbs reaching for her like claws, and something moves between them — a shape she cannot fix, a presence that bears down on her like a weight she cannot get out from under. "Celenneth." Linnea's voice, far off and echoing — but when she turns there is no Linnea, only more shadow. A guttural whisper crawls the air, saying her name in a voice that lifts the hair on her arms, and the shape swells until it blots out the sky. "You cannot outrun the dark," it tells her, and it is not a threat but a certainty.*

She came back to herself gasping, the firelight harsh on her tear-streaked face, her hand fisted white in Ellarien's sleeve. "The Shadow," she rasped. "It's coming."[^s-feystone]

Across the fire Linnea's giddy laughter faltered. "What shadow?"

Ellarien's smile had gone. She guided Celenneth down to sit, her grip firm and kind. "Whatever you saw, it hasn't happened," she said. "Feystone's gift doesn't lie. But it doesn't quite tell the truth, either."

Celenneth stared into the flames, the warmth of the smoke gone, and only a cold certainty left where it had been: the dark they were running from was nearer than she had let herself believe.

Linnea reached for her, her hand hovering at Celenneth's shoulder. "It wasn't real. It was the smoke. You're safe here."

Celenneth went rigid under the touch and shrugged it off as though it burned. "I don't need comfort," she said, harder than she meant. "Real or not — what I saw was a warning. I can't set it aside."

Linnea drew back, her mouth thinning. "Fine. But you don't have to face it alone." When Celenneth gave no answer she sighed and went to her bedroll and lay down with her back to the fire.

The night dragged. Celenneth stayed by the flames, the whisper going round and round in her — *you cannot outrun the dark* — poking at the embers until they swirled up and died in the black. After a long while Thalanar broke the quiet without looking up from the blade he was sharpening. "You won't find answers in the fire."

"No," she said. "But I might in keeping watch."

He snorted. "You'll find exhaustion. A thing like what you saw feeds on it. Sleep, ranger."

"Not yet. I'll take the first watch."

His mouth twitched. "Stubborn," he muttered. "Fine. Just don't let it eat you alive."

She sat the watch alone, turning the vision over for meaning, and when Thalanar came to relieve her hours later she only said "your turn" and brushed past him to her bedroll, her shoulders tight. Sleep came slow, and thin, and she knew her strength would not hold forever against nights like this. But for now the fire burned, and Thalanar stood over it, and that was watch enough to close her eyes to.

[^r-hart]: **Hunting** — Celenneth tracks and takes the legendary great hart of the Woodland (a beast none had caught) with a single clean shot. Meat shared for the road; the antlers gifted back to the Elves.
[^s-feystone]: **Shadow — feystone's gift.** An Elvish visionary pipeweed. Linnea's draw brings harmless euphoria; Celenneth's turns prophetic and dark — a vision of the Shadow and the certainty of pursuit, rendered in the signposted vision mode (present tense, italics). Shadow gained; a true warning wrapped in the smoke's unreliability.
