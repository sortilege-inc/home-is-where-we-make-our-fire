# Chapter 9: A Morning Swim

*Book 4*

*Narquelië 18, 2965*

Celenneth and Linnea awoke slowly, the morning light filtering through the leaves above, casting soft dappled patterns across the large, beautiful bed they found themselves in. The bedding was a luxurious blend of silken sheets and thick, plush blankets, and the two of them lay entwined in each other's arms, their bodies still warm from the night before. The room was open to the forest, the soft rustling of leaves and the gentle murmur of the nearby stream creating a tranquil ambiance.

Celenneth opened her eyes first, her gaze landing on Linnea's peaceful face, her hair spread out across the pillow like a halo. She smiled with affection as she brushed a stray strand of hair away from Linnea's face, her fingers lingering against her cheek. Linnea stirred, her eyes fluttering open, a smile curving her lips as she looked up. There was a moment of quiet, shared contentment as they lay there, basking in the warmth of each other's presence.

The sound of soft footsteps drew their attention, and they turned to see Arannis and Eryndor approaching, each carrying a tray laden with fruits, honey, and delicate pastries. The two elves smiled warmly, their eyes filled with a gentle fondness as they knelt beside the bed. "Good morning," Arannis murmured, her voice soft, melodic. "We thought you might be hungry after such a long night."

Eryndor offered a piece of fruit to Linnea, holding it to her lips, his eyes twinkling with mischief. "A taste of the forest to begin your day," he said, his voice a low purr. Linnea blushed slightly, her lips parting as she accepted the offering, the sweet juice bursting on her tongue. She closed her eyes, savoring the taste, her gaze meeting Eryndor's as she smiled.

Arannis turned her attention to Celenneth, offering her a slice of golden peach, her fingers brushing against Celenneth's lips as she fed her. "There is no rush today," she said, her voice barely above a whisper. "The forest is yours to enjoy, and we are here to attend to your every need."

Each bite was a decadent pleasure, the fruits ripe and bursting with flavor, the honey rich and fragrant. Eryndor and Arannis took their time, their touches gentle, their smiles soft, their presence a comforting reminder of the intimacy they had shared the night before. Celenneth and Linnea relaxed into the moment, their bodies still entwined, their hearts full.

Once the trays were empty, Arannis set them aside, a smile playing on her lips. "Come," she said, her eyes returning to Celenneth and Linnea. "There is a stream nearby, perfect for washing away the night and refreshing your spirits." She stood, extending a hand to Celenneth.

Eryndor offered his hand to Linnea, his smile broad and inviting. "The water is cool, and the morning is young," he said. "Let us show you the beauty of our forest in the light of day." Linnea took his hand, her fingers curling around his as she rose from the bed, her body still feeling the warmth of the night before, her heart light.

They were led down a winding path, the forest around them alive with the sounds of birdsong and the gentle rustle of leaves. The air was crisp, fresh, the scent of damp earth and blooming flowers filling their senses. The stream came into view, its waters clear and sparkling in the morning light, the gentle current creating ripples that danced across the surface.

Arannis released Celenneth's hand, her fingers moving to untie the sash of her robe, her eyes never leaving Celenneth's. "The water is cold, but it will wake you, refresh you," she said, her voice soft, inviting. She let her own robe fall to the ground, her body bathed in the golden light of the morning, her smile warm as she stepped into the stream, the water lapping at her ankles.

Eryndor followed suit, his robe slipping from his shoulders as he turned to Linnea, his gaze filled with admiration. "Join us," he said, his voice barely louder than a whisper, his hand extended to her. Linnea glanced at Celenneth, a smile tugging at her lips as she let her own robe fall, her fingers brushing against Celenneth's before she stepped forward, taking Eryndor's hand.

Celenneth watched for a moment, her heart swelling with a mixture of love and wonder. She let her robe slip from her shoulders, her body bare in the morning light, the cool air brushing against her skin as she stepped into the stream, the water cool, refreshing.

The water flowed around them, clear and cold, the sensation invigorating. Arannis and Eryndor moved with an easy grace, their laughter light, melodic, as they splashed each other, their joy infectious. Celenneth and Linnea found themselves laughing, their worries washed away by the cool water, the warmth of the elves, and the beauty of the forest around them.

They swam together, their bodies moving effortlessly through the water, the sunlight filtering through the leaves above, casting a golden glow over the stream. Arannis and Eryndor stayed close, their touches gentle, their eyes filled with affection as they led Celenneth and Linnea through the water, their voices soft, filled with laughter and warmth.

Arannis began to hum a melody, her voice sweet and lilting, blending perfectly with the sounds of the forest around them. She turned to Celenneth and Linnea, her eyes bright with joy. "This is an old elvish song," she said, her voice carrying over the gentle rush of the stream. "It is about how true enjoyment is found in the surprise of the unexpected."

Arannis sang, her voice filling the glade. The words were in Silvan, flowing and beautiful, and as she sang, she encouraged Celenneth and Linnea to join in, teaching them the words as she went, her laughter warm and gentle whenever they stumbled over the unfamiliar sounds. Linnea's voice was soft, hesitant at first, but with Arannis's encouragement, it grew stronger, blending with Celenneth's and Arannis's, their voices weaving together, the melody echoing across the water.

Eryndor watched them and swam closer, resting his hand on Linnea's back, his presence a steadying force. "Surprise brings delight," Arannis translated, her voice soft, her eyes meeting Celenneth's. "The unexpected is where joy lives."

The song ended with laughter, the four of them splashing each other, the cool water sparkling in the sunlight, their voices mingling with the rustle of the leaves and the distant calls of birds. They lingered in the stream, the water flowing around them, their bodies relaxed, their hearts light, the beauty of the morning filling them with a sense of peace and connection.

After some time, Arannis led them back to the shore. They gathered their robes, wrapping themselves in the soft fabric, the cool air brushing against their damp skin as they made their way back to the woodland halls.

Having returned, Eryndor gestured for them to follow him to a spacious room adorned with cushions and soft rugs, the scent of flowers filling the air. In the center of the room stood a beautifully carved harp, its strings shimmering in the light. Eryndor moved to sit beside it, his fingers brushing over the strings, a soft, melodic sound filling the room.

Arannis settled onto the cushions, pulling Celenneth down beside her, her arms wrapping around her, her fingers brushing through her hair. Linnea lay down beside them, her head resting on Celenneth's lap, her eyes closing as she listened to the gentle music. Eryndor's fingers moved with an effortless grace, each note resonating through the room, the melody soft and soothing.

The music wrapped around them, the gentle rise and fall of the notes creating a sense of calm, of peace. Arannis's fingers traced idle patterns along Celenneth's arm, her touch light, comforting. Linnea's breathing slowed, her body relaxing against Celenneth's, her heart filled with a deep sense of contentment, of belonging.

Eryndor's  music was a gift, a flaunting of the beauty elves were capable of, but elegant enough to feel earned and connecting, and not an act of hubris. The room warmed as morning turned to afternoon as they were, lulled by the sounds of the harp, and the steady, calming rhythm of their breathing.
