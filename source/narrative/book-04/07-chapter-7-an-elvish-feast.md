# Chapter 7: An Elvish Feast

*Book 4*

The evening began with a flourish, the soft glow of lanterns hanging in the branches above, casting a warm, golden light over the pavilion. Eryndor and Arannis led Celenneth and Linnea to a long table set with intricate dishes and carved goblets, everything radiating the delicate artistry of the elves. The twenty-course menu was presented with an effortless grace, each dish demonstrating a facet of the brilliance of the forest.

The first course was a delicate salad of fresh herbs and woodland flowers, the flavors subtle and refreshing. It was followed by thin slices of smoked river trout, served with a sauce made from crushed berries and honey. As each course arrived, so too did the music—songs of the forest, lilting melodies played on flutes and strings that seemed to blend perfectly with the rustle of leaves and the distant calls of night birds.

The third course was a light broth, infused with wild mushrooms and seasoned with herbs that brought warmth to their bellies. It was served with thin, crispy bread, still warm from the oven. The flavors were rich, earthy, and comforting. As they sipped from their bowls, Eryndor leaned forward, his eyes glinting in the lantern light. "You see, every taste here is a piece of our forest," he said, his voice low and inviting, his gaze lingering on Linnea. "We wish to share it all with you."

Next came small roasted birds, their skin crisp, glazed with a sweet wine reduction, and served alongside roasted roots that had been pulled from the forest floor just that morning. Arannis smiled as she placed a small portion on Celenneth's plate, her fingers grazing Celenneth's hand. "Taste this," she murmured, her voice barely above a whisper. "It is said to bring good fortune to those who dine together."

The wine flowed freely, golden and fragrant, and with each sip, Celenneth and Linnea felt the tension of their journey melt away. The elves, too, seemed to grow more relaxed, their laughter growing louder, their smiles growing wider. Arannis took a goblet and raised it to the two travelers. "To love, and the courage it brings," she said, her voice rich with meaning, her gaze lingering on Celenneth and Linnea before she drank.

In between courses, Eryndor and Arannis took turns singing songs, their voices clear and pure, carrying with them the magic of the forest. Arannis sang of ancient loves, of loss and reunion beneath the stars, her voice haunting, her eyes catching Celenneth's as if speaking directly to her heart. When her song ended, Eryndor stood, his eyes bright with mischief. He sang of joy and revelry, of laughter shared beneath the boughs of the trees, and as his voice rose, he stepped forward, taking Linnea's hand.

"Come, dance with me," he said, his grin wide and inviting. Linnea hesitated, her eyes darting to Celenneth, but at Celenneth's encouraging nod, she let Eryndor pull her to her feet. They moved together, Eryndor guiding her with an easy confidence, his laughter infectious as he spun her beneath the lantern light. Arannis watched for a moment before she, too, stood, extending her hand to Celenneth. "Will you join me?" she asked, her smile warm.

Celenneth allowed herself to be led. The music swelled, the rhythm quickening as they danced, their feet moving lightly over the forest floor. Arannis moved with a grace that seemed almost ethereal, her silver hair catching the light as she twirled, her laughter soft and melodic. Celenneth felt her heart lift as she watched Linnea laugh as she was spun by Eryndor.

The fifth course was served as they returned to their seats—thin slices of venison, smoked and spiced, arranged delicately on a bed of greens. The taste was rich, almost decadent, and the elves watched eagerly as Celenneth and Linnea took their first bites. Arannis leaned close to Celenneth, her voice soft, almost conspiratorial. "Venison of this quality is reserved for honored guests," she whispered, her fingers lingering on Celenneth's arm. "It is said to embolden the spirit."

The sixth course brought tiny pastries filled with goat cheese and herbs, the crusts flaky and golden. Eryndor took one and offered it to Linnea, holding it to her lips until she took a bite, his eyes never leaving hers. "You must try everything," he said, his voice thick with meaning. "To taste the forest is to know us, truly."

As the dinner continued, the courses became more surprising and decadent. A platter of roasted game, seasoned with wild herbs and served with golden mushrooms, followed by tender greens drizzled in fragrant oils. The elves brought forth fruits unlike any Celenneth or Linnea had ever seen—glowing with an inner light, their sweetness intoxicating. The fruit juice lingered on their lips, and the elves did not hesitate to lean close, their fingers brushing against Celenneth and Linnea as they wiped away the drops, their touches lingering.

The eleventh course was a fragrant stew, served in small bowls carved from hollowed wood. The stew was thick, filled with vegetables and chunks of meat, the spices warming them from the inside. Arannis spooned a bit of the broth, offering it to Celenneth, her eyes locked on hers. "Trust me," she whispered, her lips curving into a soft smile as Celenneth accepted the offering.

Eryndor leaned close to Linnea, his breath warm against her ear as he spoke. "You dance beautifully," he murmured, his fingers brushing against her arm. "Perhaps later, you will teach me more of your ways, and I will teach you more of ours." His eyes sparkled, his meaning unmistakable. Linnea found herself smiling, the warmth of the wine loosening her inhibitions. "Perhaps," she replied, her voice low, her gaze meeting his with a hint of challenge.

Arannis, too, had grown bolder, her hand resting lightly on Celenneth's arm, her gaze unwavering. "The forest has seen many lovers, but few as radiant as you," she whispered, her lips close to Celenneth's ear. "Stay with us tonight, and let us show you all the pleasures our realm has to offer." Celenneth hesitated, her heart pounding, but the allure in Arannis's gaze was undeniable, her words tempting. Her eyes caught Linnea's, and in them she saw a confidence, and an openness, and she relaxed and returned the gaze with one of her own.

The fifteenth course consisted of spiced nuts and dried fruits, each bite a burst of flavor, sweet and salty. Eryndor fed Linnea a dried fig, his fingers brushing her lips as he did so. "You must not shy away from pleasure," he said, his voice almost a purr. "It is what makes us alive."

By the time the seventeenth course arrived, Celenneth and Linnea found themselves laughing easily, the wine and the warmth of the evening bringing a sense of freedom they had not known in some time. The elves were relentless in their attentions, their flirtations now barely veiled, their touches growing bolder. Arannis traced a finger along Celenneth's jaw, her eyes filled with promise. "Do you feel it? The magic of the forest, the way it calls to us? Tonight, we are bound to it, and to each other."

Linnea, emboldened by the wine, leaned into Eryndor as he spoke softly to her, his words a mix of elvish and common tongue, his meaning clear even if the words were not. "You are like a flame," he said, his fingers brushing the back of her neck. "Bright, untamed. I wish to feel your warmth, to know your fire."

The eighteenth course was a dessert of honey cakes, drizzled with a syrup made from forest berries. The sweetness was almost overwhelming, the flavors bursting, and as they finished, Arannis poured more wine, her gaze never leaving Celenneth. "There is more to come," she whispered, her voice low, intimate. "If you wish it."

The nineteenth course was a platter of cheeses, sharp and tangy, served with slices of dark bread. Eryndor broke off a piece and offered it to Linnea, his eyes filled with an intensity that made her breath catch. "Taste this, and know it is given with admiration," he said, his fingers brushing her lips once more.

The final course was brought out—a shimmering concoction of chilled fruit, served in delicate crystal cups, the juice glistening like liquid sunlight. Arannis lifted her cup, her eyes locking onto Celenneth's. "To new beginnings," she said, her voice soft, filled with meaning.

The wine flowed, the music played on, and the night grew deeper. The laughter of the elves echoed around them, the air thick with the scent of blossoms and the warmth of the fire. The forest seemed to hold its breath, watching as the line between hospitality and seduction blurred, the promise of pleasure lingering in every glance, every smile, every whispered word. Celenneth and Linnea felt themselves drawn in, the magic of the forest and the allure of their hosts weaving a spell that was impossible to resist.
