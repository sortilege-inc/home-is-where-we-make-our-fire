# Chapter 2: Guidance

*Book 4*

*Narquelië 12, 2965*

The journey day toward Mirkwood began under clear skies, the Forest Running glinting in the sunlight as Celenneth and Linnea rode northward. Nimroch's steady pace and the gentle rustle of the wind made for a calm start to their journey. As they traveled, the outline of Mirkwood loomed closer, its dark edges a sharp contrast to the open plains.

As Celenneth and Linnea approached a quiet bend in the river, they spotted a lone figure standing by the water's edge. The traveler was cloaked, their face obscured, and a pack lay at their feet. The figure raised a hand in greeting but made no move to approach.

Celenneth signaled for Linnea to hold back as she dismounted Nimroch, her hand resting lightly on her sword. The cloaked figure didn't move, their posture calm but not entirely reassuring.

"What brings you here?" Celenneth called, her voice steady.

The traveler lifted their hood slightly, revealing an older man with a weathered face and sharp eyes. "Just a wanderer," he said, his tone smooth but cautious. "The road is long, and the river offers good company. And you? Heading into the woods, are you?"

Linnea stayed mounted, her hand on her bowstring, ready in case of trouble. "What's it to you?" she asked sharply.

The man chuckled, his gaze flicking between them. "Nothing at all. Just offering some advice: the woods aren't kind to those who enter unprepared."

Celenneth narrowed her eyes, studying him. "And what would you know of Mirkwood?"

"A fair bit," the man replied, leaning on a walking stick. "Enough to know the paths and avoid the shadows. If you'd like guidance, I'd be happy to offer it—for a price."

Celenneth stepped closer, her keen eyes never leaving the man's face. "I don't carry much in the way of coin," she said plainly. "But if your offer is genuine, we might have something else to exchange."

The traveler tilted his head, intrigued. "I would accept something useful for the road. A blade well-forged, a vial of elvish medicine, or a tale well-told."

Celenneth considered his words and glanced back at Linnea, who shrugged slightly. "We've supplies," Linnea murmured. "And you have songs, don't you?"

Celenneth nodded and turned back to the man. "I'll offer you something more enduring than coin," she said. "A song—a tale to keep you company on your own long road."

The man's expression shifted, a flicker of genuine interest crossing his face. "A song, you say?" He chuckled. "I know all the old songs, so you'll have to give me something new."

Celenneth considered the man's request, her eyes narrowing thoughtfully. She didn't often sing in the presence of others, but she felt the weight of this moment. After a brief pause, she nodded. "I'll sing you a story worth the road."

She began in a low, resonant tone, the melody carrying both grandeur and sorrow. The song spoke of Lif, the brave dwarf who fought against Malech, the one-eyed orc who had terrorized Moria. Her words painted a vivid picture of Lif's courage, his blade gleaming in the dark, his spirit unyielding even in the face of certain death.

But the song shifted, its melody darkening. Celenneth sang of the tragic battle, of how Lif's valor could not overcome the sheer might of his enemy. She told of the final moments, the spear that pierced Lif's side, and the quiet stillness that followed. Her voice trembled briefly, but she pressed on, the tale bearing the weight of sacrifice and the cost of heroism.

As the final notes faded, Linnea stared at Celenneth, her expression a mixture of awe and curiosity. "I've never heard that before," she said softly. "Malech and Lif; did you learn that song from the dwarves?"

Celenneth met her gaze, her tone quieter now. "I wrote that song in Lif's memory—as a memorial, and a reminder."

Linnea nodded slowly, her voice barely above a whisper. "It's beautiful, and... so sad."

The older man listened intently, his sharp features softened by the weight of the tale. When Celenneth finished, he let out a long breath, his eyes widening slightly. "You mean you were there? You saw all this firsthand?" he asked, his tone a mix of astonishment and respect.

Celenneth nodded, her gaze distant. "I fought beside Lif. His courage deserved to be remembered."

The man shook his head, a look of deep admiration on his face. "A story as rich and deep as the halls themselves," he murmured. "You've honored me, ranger. And I'll honor you with good counsel."

He gestured toward the looming line of Mirkwood in the distance. "The left fork keeps you clear of the worst bogs. If you hear the trees creak louder than the wind, move across the river. And beware of shadows that move without reason."

With a final nod, he turned and disappeared down the trail, leaving the two women alone by the river.

As they remounted Nimroch, Linnea looked at Celenneth curiously. "You've seen so much, haven't you?" she asked, her voice quiet but full of wonder. "Not just places, but... stories. Do you ever wonder how much of what we know will be forgotten, like Moria?"

Celenneth glanced at her, her voice measured. "That's why we remember. Even the sad stories. Because forgetting them... means losing the lessons, the places, and the people who lived there."

Linnea nodded thoughtfully as they set off toward the forest's edge, the melody of Moria lingering in her mind.
