# Chapter 10: Audience

*Narquelië 18, 2965*

Late afternoon light came slanting through the high branches when the summons reached them, and Eryndor and Arannis walked them back through the winding ways to the great pavilion at the heart of the halls. Thalion stood at its center, his gaze fixed on something far past the borders of his realm, and turned to them as they entered — and there was a warmth in his eyes now that had not been there at their first meeting.

"Celenneth of the North, Linnea of Bree," he said. "Welcome again. Your message has been weighed, and I have taken counsel. I will share my mind with you." He gestured them closer.

"We understand the threat that gathers in the south-west," he went on, his eyes on Celenneth. "The Shadow of Dol Guldur wakes, and it is not Dale alone that stands in its path. The forest itself is stirring, and we must be ready for what comes." His gaze moved to Linnea, and softened. "I have resolved, with my council, to stand with the Men of Dale. Our scouts will watch the Mirkwood; our bows will hold the forest; and our warriors will march beside Dale's, should the need come."

Relief went through Celenneth like warmth after cold. "Thank you, my lord," she said. "Your strength will mean much to Dale, and to all who stand against the dark."

Thalion inclined his head. "The woodland must stay strong, and the alliances of the Free Peoples must hold. I have heard, too, that a like plea must be carried to Rohan — that the Horse-lords be warned of what stirs." He turned to them, purpose in his face. "I will speed your way. There are paths known to my people that will carry you swifter and safer than any road."

Linnea stepped forward, her eyes bright. "We're grateful, Lord Thalion. Rohan must know what's coming. The alliance of the Free Peoples has to hold now, more than ever."

"Indeed, brave Linnea." He smiled. "You will go with the blessing of Thalion and of Bain both, and of the forest besides. My scouts will see you safe to the borders of Rohan; from there I trust your own courage to carry the rest." He raised a hand toward Eryndor and Arannis. "In the meantime, rest, and gather your strength. There is time yet before you must depart, and I would have you leave my halls renewed." His eyes warmed. "You are friends of the woodland now. You will always find sanctuary here."[^r-alliance]

Eryndor stepped between the two women and took a hand of each. "Come," he said softly. "There is still much of the forest to enjoy before your road takes you on."

[^r-alliance]: **Undertaking — the Woodland alliance secured.** Thalion pledges the Woodland Realm to stand with Dale: scouts, archers, and warriors against the muster of Dol Guldur, plus safe-conduct through his lands and toward Rohan. The major Fellowship goal of the book achieved — and a new errand set: carry the warning to the Horse-lords of Rohan.
