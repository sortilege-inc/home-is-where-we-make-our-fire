# Chapter 10: Audience

*Book 4*

The late afternoon sun filtered through the high branches of the ancient trees, casting long shadows across the forest floor as Celenneth and Linnea were summoned once more to the heart of the woodland halls. The great pavilion of Lord Thalion stood before them, its natural architecture woven seamlessly from the living branches of the forest, bathed in the warm, dappled light of the woodland. The air was filled with the scent of wildflowers and the distant murmur of the river, a sense of both power and serenity emanating from the place.

They had been summoned, and Eryndor and Arannis escorted them, their presence a comfort as they moved through the winding paths of the forest, their steps quiet. As they approached the pavilion, Lord Thalion stood at its center, his gaze focused on something far beyond the borders of his realm, his expression serious but composed. He turned to face them as they entered, his golden hair catching the light, his eyes filled with a warmth that had not been present during their first meeting.

"Celenneth of the North, Linnea of Bree," he greeted, his voice carrying the gentle authority of the forest itself. "Welcome again to my halls. Your message has been heard, and I have taken council with those who guide our realm. I wish to share my decision with you." He gestured for them to approach, and they stepped forward.

"We understand the threat that gathers in the southwest," Thalion continued, his eyes meeting Celenneth's. "The shadow of Dol Guldur grows once more, and it is not only Dale that finds itself in peril. The forest is waking, and we must prepare for what lies ahead." He paused, his gaze shifting to Linnea, a gentle smile touching his lips. "I have spoken with the council, and we have resolved to stand with the men of Dale. Our scouts will monitor the Mirkwood, our bows will defend the forest, and our warriors will join those of Dale should the need arise."

Celenneth felt a wave of relief wash over her, her heart swelling with gratitude. "Thank you, my lord," she said, her voice steady, though the weight of his words made her feel both humbled and strengthened. "Your support will mean much to the people of Dale, and to all who stand against the shadow."

Thalion nodded, his gaze shifting to the ancient trees that surrounded them. "The woodland must remain strong, and our alliances must hold fast. I have also heard that there is a similar ask to be made of Rohan, to warn the Horse-lords of the growing danger." He turned back to them, his eyes filled with purpose. "I will lend my support to your journey. There are paths known to us that will make your way swifter and safer."

Linnea stepped forward, her eyes shining with with accomplishment. "We are grateful, Lord Thalion," she said, her voice filled with emotion. "The people of Rohan must know what is coming. The alliance of the Free Peoples must hold, now more than ever."

Thalion smiled broadly. "Indeed, brave Linnea," he said. "You will carry with you the blessings of Thalion and Bain, and the blessing of the forest. You will find safe passage through our lands, and our scouts will see you safely to the borders of Rohan. From there, I trust in your courage and determination to see this journey through."

He raised a hand, gesturing for Eryndor and Arannis to step forward. "In the meantime, rest and gather your strength. There is still time before you must depart, and we would see you leave our realm refreshed and ready for what lies ahead." He paused, his eyes warm as he looked upon them. "You are friends of the woodland, and you will always find sanctuary here."

Eryndor stepped between Celenneth and Linnea and took their hands. "Come," he said softly. "There is still much of the forest to enjoy before your journey continues."
