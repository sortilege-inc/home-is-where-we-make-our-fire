# Chapter 12: Brynja's Fall

*Lótessë 20, 2969*

The sun hung high, dappling gold through the rustling canopy, the forest alive with birdsong and the distant hum of water. Brynja skipped ahead, her dark curls bouncing as she darted tree to tree, her small bow across her back beside a pouch brimming with treasures — a smooth stone, a bright red leaf, a tiny bird's nest she'd promised to show Linnea later. Celenneth walked a short way behind, her eyes on the wood; Linnea brought up the rear, her gaze on Brynja with fond amusement and a mild worry both.

"Stay where we can see you," Linnea called.

"I am, Mama! I'm looking for tracks!"

Celenneth chuckled, watching the girl's eager search. "She's getting bolder," Linnea murmured. "Almost too bold."

"She's curious," Celenneth said. "That's a good thing — so long as we guide it."

The sound of water grew until they reached a narrow, sparkling stream, and Brynja skidded to a stop at its edge, peering down. "Look! Fish!"

Celenneth knelt beside her. "Minnows. See how they dart? You'd need a quick hand for one of those." Then her gaze sharpened on the far bank, where a faint set of prints pressed into the mud. "There. A fox, maybe. Recent."

"Can we follow it?" Brynja's excitement bubbled over.

Celenneth rose and put out her hand. "We cross here. The water's shallow, but the rocks are slippery. Step careful." Brynja gripped her hand, face set with determination, and Celenneth crossed first, finding sure purchase on the slick stones, then turned and held out her hands. Brynja took the first rock confidently, wobbled, recovered; Linnea watched close, her hands hovering. "Good," Celenneth encouraged. "Next — slowly." Brynja grinned and took another step, and another —

— and on the third rock her boot slipped, her arms flailing. "Mama!" she cried, high with panic, and pitched sideways into the stream with a sharp, bone-jarring thud against the rocky bank.

"Brynja!" Linnea was already moving, splashing through the water.

Celenneth dropped to her knees, her hands searching the child's small body with a precision she wished she did not have — for her own hands had set a broken leg once, alone in Fangorn, and knew the shape of this too well. Brynja whimpered, tears streaming, but did not scream. "It's her leg," Celenneth said, even, though her voice was tight. Her fingers traced the shin, feeling for the break. "It's broken."

Linnea dropped down and cupped Brynja's face. "Oh, my love — does it hurt?"

Brynja's breath shuddered. "It hurts, Mama."

Celenneth's calm held, though Linnea could see the tightness at her eyes. "It's a clean break. We splint it, and get her home. Hold her still while I find sticks." She scanned the ground and returned in moments with two sturdy branches, already tearing strips from her cloak with deft hands. "Brynja," she murmured, "this will hurt. But I'll be quick." Brynja nodded bravely and gripped Linnea's hand, and Celenneth set the splint with precise, efficient movements. The girl let out a sharp cry but did not wail, her trust in them holding her steady.[^r-brynja-leg]

When it was secure Celenneth gathered her up gently. Brynja shivered but rested her head on her mother's shoulder, her breath still unsteady. "She'll need to rest, and stay off it," Celenneth said, softer now. "But she'll heal."

Linnea pressed a kiss to the damp forehead. "You were so brave, my little bird. We'll take care of you."

Brynja sniffled. "I didn't mean to fall."

"It wasn't your fault," Celenneth said, brushing a strand from her face. "Accidents happen. What matters is you're safe now." And as they made their way back through the quiet wood, Linnea kept close with a hand on Brynja's back, anchoring herself in the girl's steady breathing, her heart still pounding with the memory of the fall — but Celenneth's calm strength held them all together. They were a team; together they could face anything. Even broken bones, and the fears that came with them.

[^r-brynja-leg]: **Wound** — crossing a stream, Brynja slips and breaks her lower leg (a clean fracture). Celenneth sets and splints it on the spot — the same craft, and the same injury, she once tended on herself in Fangorn. Brynja bears it bravely; she will heal with rest.
