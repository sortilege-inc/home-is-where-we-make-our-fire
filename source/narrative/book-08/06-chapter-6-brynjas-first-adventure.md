# Chapter 6: Brynja’s First Adventure

*Book 8*

*Víressë,  2969*

The morning dawned crisp and bright, the sky a canvas of pale blue streaked with golden sunlight. Brynja stood at the edge of the small grove near their home, her tiny frame bundled in a sturdy cloak. Her curls peeked out from under her hood, and her bright eyes sparkled with excitement. She held a small bow in her hands—crafted by Celenneth just for her.

Celenneth knelt beside her, adjusting the strap of a small quiver slung over Brynja’s shoulder. “Remember,” Celenneth said, her tone both patient and firm, “this isn’t a game. We’re going to follow the tracks, stay quiet, and keep our eyes open. If anything feels wrong, you come to me right away.”

Brynja nodded earnestly, her expression serious. “I understand, Mama.”

Celenneth smiled, brushing a stray curl from Brynja’s face. “Good. Then let’s see what we can find.”

Their journey began with a gentle trek into the woods, the quiet hum of the forest surrounding them. Celenneth moved with practiced ease, her steps light and deliberate, while Brynja mimicked her mother’s movements, though her small boots often crunched noisily against the fallen leaves.

“Look here,” Celenneth whispered after a while, crouching low and pointing to a patch of soft earth. “See these marks? What do you think made them?”

Brynja squatted beside her, her little brow furrowing in concentration. “A deer?”

Celenneth nodded, impressed. “That’s right. And see how the tracks go this way?” She gestured further into the woods. “That’s the trail we’ll follow.”

They moved on, the thrill of tracking filling Brynja with excitement. Occasionally, Celenneth would pause to show her daughter a plant or a sign of wildlife, explaining how each piece fit into the larger tapestry of the wilds. Brynja soaked up every word, her small hands reaching out to touch the bark of trees or pluck a stray feather from the ground.

As the sun climbed higher, they reached a small clearing where the tracks disappeared near a shallow stream. Brynja knelt at the water’s edge, peering into the clear, babbling current.

“Do you think the deer stopped to drink here?” she asked.

“Probably,” Celenneth said, watching her with a fond smile. “And it might still be nearby. Do you want to try and find it?”

Brynja nodded eagerly, but before they could move on, the quiet was broken by the sound of rustling in the underbrush. Brynja froze, her eyes widening as she clutched her bow tightly. Celenneth’s hand came to rest gently on her shoulder, her expression calm but alert.

“It’s all right,” Celenneth murmured. “Stay behind me.”

The rustling grew louder, and moments later, a rabbit bolted from the brush, its soft fur a blur as it darted past them. Brynja let out a breath she didn’t realize she’d been holding, her wide eyes turning to Celenneth.

“It was just a rabbit!” she exclaimed, half-laughing, half-relieved.

Celenneth chuckled softly, her hand ruffling Brynja’s hair. “The forest is full of surprises. You handled that well.”

They spent the rest of the day exploring, Brynja’s confidence growing with each small discovery. By the time they returned home, her cheeks were flushed with excitement, and her quiver was filled with treasures—feathers, smooth stones, and even a tiny antler she’d found near a tree.

“Did I do good, Mama?” she asked as they reached the edge of the grove, her voice hopeful.

“You did very well,” Celenneth said, lifting Brynja into her arms. “You’re a natural.”

Brynja beamed, her arms wrapping tightly around her mother’s neck. It was her first adventure, but it wouldn’t be her last. For Celenneth, it was a reminder of the joy of sharing the wilds with someone she loved, and for Brynja, it was the start of a lifetime of discovery.

Brynja sat cross-legged on the rug by the fire, her cheeks still rosy from the cold and her voice animated as she recounted the day’s events. Linnea leaned back in her chair, smiling softly as she listened, her hands busy darning a small tear in one of Brynja’s cloaks.

“And then, Mama said we were following deer tracks,” Brynja began, her hands waving excitedly. “Big deer tracks! Like this big!” She stretched her arms wide, clearly overestimating the size of the animal.

Linnea raised an eyebrow, suppressing a laugh. “That’s quite the deer.”

Brynja nodded solemnly, her curls bouncing. “Yes! But we didn’t see it because it was too sneaky. It must have been hiding in the trees. But I found other things! Like this feather!” She held up a small gray feather, her eyes shining. “Do you think it’s from a hawk? Mama says it might be.”

Linnea set the cloak aside, leaning forward to inspect the feather. “It could be,” she said, her voice encouraging. “It’s a very fine feather, Brynja.”

Brynja beamed, clearly proud of her find. “And then, we were at the stream, and I was looking for more tracks—because I’m very good at finding tracks, you know—and then we heard something in the bushes!”

She paused dramatically, her eyes widening as she leaned closer to Linnea. “It was SO loud, like ‘RUSTLE RUSTLE,’ and I thought it might be a monster!”

Linnea gasped, playing along. “A monster? In the forest?”

Brynja nodded earnestly. “Yes! But Mama said to stay behind her, and I did, because I’m very brave. And then—guess what it was?”

“What?” Linnea asked, her eyes twinkling.

“A rabbit!” Brynja exclaimed, throwing her arms up. “It was so fast, like ‘WHOOSH,’ and it ran right past us! I wasn’t scared, though. Not even a little.”

Linnea chuckled, reaching out to tuck a stray curl behind Brynja’s ear. “Not even a little?”

Brynja hesitated, then grinned sheepishly. “Okay, maybe a little. But only at first!”

Linnea laughed, pulling Brynja into her lap. “You’re braver than I was at your age, that’s for sure. Did you have fun?”

Brynja nodded enthusiastically. “Yes! Mama showed me everything—tracks and plants and even where a deer might sleep. And I found lots of treasures!” She held up a small pouch, shaking it so the contents rattled.

Linnea kissed the top of her head, her heart swelling with pride and love. “It sounds like you had quite the adventure.”

Brynja snuggled closer, her voice growing softer. “Mama said I’m a natural. Do you think I can go again soon?”

“I’m sure Mama would love to take you,” Linnea said, her smile warm. “But next time, maybe I’ll come too.”

Brynja’s eyes lit up at the idea. “Yes! Then we can all go together!”

Linnea held her close, her gaze drifting to the door where Celenneth had just entered, her own smile mirroring Linnea’s. Brynja’s retelling might have been full of embellishments, but the joy in her voice was unmistakable—a reminder of the small, precious moments that made their family whole.
