# Chapter 6: Brynja's First Adventure

*Víressë 18, 2969*

The morning dawned crisp and bright, and Brynja stood at the edge of the grove near their house, bundled in a sturdy cloak, her curls peeking from under the hood, a small bow — carved by Celenneth just for her — clutched in her hands. Celenneth knelt to settle the little quiver over her shoulder. "Remember," she said, patient but firm, "this isn't a game. We follow the tracks, stay quiet, keep our eyes open. If anything feels wrong, you come to me at once."

Brynja nodded, solemn. "I understand, Mama."

"Good. Let's see what we can find."

They went into the wood, Celenneth moving light and deliberate, Brynja mimicking her though her small boots crunched noisily on the leaves. "Look here," Celenneth whispered after a time, crouching over a patch of soft earth. "See these marks? What made them?"

Brynja squatted beside her, brow furrowed. "A deer?"

"That's right. And see how they go this way?" She gestured on into the trees. "That's the trail we follow." They moved on, and now and then she stopped to show her daughter a plant or a sign of wildlife, and Brynja drank it all in, reaching to touch the bark of trees or pluck a stray feather from the ground. Where the tracks faded near a shallow stream, Brynja knelt at the water's edge. "Did the deer stop to drink here?"

"Likely. And it may still be near. Do you want to try and find it?" Brynja nodded eagerly — but a rustling in the underbrush froze her, and Celenneth's hand came gently to her shoulder. "It's all right. Stay behind me." The rustle grew, and then a rabbit bolted from the brush and darted past them, and Brynja let out the breath she'd been holding.

"It was just a rabbit!" she exclaimed, half-laughing.

Celenneth chuckled and ruffled her hair. "The forest is full of surprises. You handled that well." They spent the rest of the day at it, Brynja's confidence growing with each small find, and by the time they turned home her cheeks were flushed and her quiver rattled with treasures — feathers, smooth stones, even a tiny shed antler. "Did I do good, Mama?" she asked at the grove's edge.

"You did very well." Celenneth lifted her into her arms. "You're a natural." Brynja beamed and wrapped her arms tight around her mother's neck.

Brynja sat cross-legged on the rug by the fire, cheeks still rosy, recounting the day with her whole body while Linnea leaned back darning a tear in one of the child's cloaks. "And then Mama said we were following deer tracks — BIG deer tracks, like this big!" She stretched her arms wide.

Linnea raised an eyebrow, biting back a laugh. "That's quite the deer."

"Yes! But we didn't see it, it was too sneaky. But I found other things! Like this feather!" She held up a small grey feather, eyes shining. "Do you think it's from a hawk? Mama says it might be."

Linnea set the cloak aside and leaned to inspect it. "It could be. It's a very fine feather, Brynja."

"And then we were at the stream, and I was looking for more tracks — because I'm very good at finding tracks — and we heard something in the bushes!" She paused, eyes widening. "It was SO loud, like *rustle rustle,* and I thought it was a monster!"

Linnea gasped, playing along. "A monster? In the forest?"

"Yes! But Mama said to stay behind her, and I did, because I'm brave. And guess what it was?"

"What?"

"A rabbit!" Brynja threw her arms up. "So fast, like *whoosh,* right past us! I wasn't scared. Not even a little." She hesitated, then grinned sheepishly. "Okay — maybe a little. But only at first!"

Linnea laughed and pulled her into her lap. "You're braver than I was at your age, that's certain. Did you have fun?"

"Yes! Mama showed me everything — tracks and plants and even where a deer might sleep. And I found lots of treasures!" She held up a little pouch and shook it so the contents rattled. Then, softer, snuggling closer: "Mama said I'm a natural. Can we go again soon?"

"I'm sure she'd love to take you," Linnea said, warm. "But next time, maybe I'll come too."

Brynja's eyes lit. "Yes! Then we all go together!"

Linnea held her close, her gaze drifting to the door where Celenneth had just come in, her own smile mirroring Linnea's. The retelling was full of embellishment, but the joy in it was true — one of the small, precious things that made the three of them whole.
