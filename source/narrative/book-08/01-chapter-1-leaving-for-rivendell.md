# Chapter 1: Leaving for Rivendell

*Súlimë 3, 2969*

The fire crackled low as Linnea sat at the table with Brynja in her lap, the child tugging idly at the edge of her green cloak, and Freowyn peeled an apple by the window, her gaze flicking now and then to the tension in the room. Celenneth lingered near the door, arms crossed, braced as if against a wind.

"I need to speak with you both," she said.

Linnea's head came up. "What is it?"

"Elrond's summons isn't something I can set aside. I've decided to go to Rivendell." She looked between them. "And I want you with me. All of you. Brynja too."

Linnea's arm tightened around the child. "The road to Rivendell is long, Celenneth. And dangerous. Why take her into that?"

"Because I can't leave her. I can't leave any of you. If Elrond's message carries the weight I think it does, I'll want you at my side."

Freowyn set the apple down, her face thoughtful and a little sad. She glanced at Brynja, then back. "I understand why you'd take your family with you. But my place isn't on that road. I'll not follow you to Rivendell."

"Freowyn—"

"Brynja has strong parents in the two of you," the older woman said gently, "and I've treasured every day I've helped raise her. But this is your journey, not mine. And she's weaned now; she doesn't need me the way she did." She smiled faintly. "You gave me something I never looked to have — the chance to be part of a child's life, if only for a while. That's a gift I'll carry always. Take care of her. Take care of each other. That's all I ask."

Celenneth stepped close and took her hand. "I can't thank you enough. For all of it."

Brynja chattered to herself, oblivious to the weight of the moment, and Linnea held her a little tighter, and Freowyn's gaze lingered on the child, wistful. "I'll see you all again someday, I hope. But for now, the road is yours."

⁂

They rode out into frost-laden hills, Celenneth at the front reading the horizon, Linnea behind with Brynja bundled and secure before her on Eorlin, the chestnut mare stepping careful and sure. The air was crisp with pine and the faint promise of snow. Brynja squealed and gripped the saddle-bow, and Linnea adjusted her scarf, murmuring, "Hush, love."

They made the journey a slow schooling for the child. One evening under a gold sunset Celenneth knelt and set a small bow in Brynja's hands, and Brynja's laughter rang out as her first arrow sailed wide and buried itself in a patch of moss. "Almost," Celenneth encouraged, adjusting her grip. "Steady, this time." Brynja squinted, tongue between her teeth, and loosed again — closer, and both women cheered. Another day, in a quiet glade, Linnea crouched with her over wild garlic and fiddlehead ferns. "These are good to eat," she said. "But always look close — some plants have dangerous cousins." Brynja nodded solemnly, brushing the leaves, while Celenneth kept her watch on the treeline, her vigilance never quite at rest.

The tension of the road boiled over one evening beside a running stream. "We cross here," Celenneth said, arms folded. "The ford's shallow enough. It saves us near a day's backtracking."

Linnea frowned as she unpacked. "That stream looks gentle, but the rains upriver make it treacherous. What if the current's stronger than it seems? Would you risk Brynja being swept away?"

"We'll manage. I'll cross first and make sure. We've lost too much time already."

"And if it isn't safe? We're not two rangers on a mission, Celenneth. We have a child to think of."

"I know that," Celenneth snapped, her voice rising. "But we can't always take the safest path. Every day we delay leaves us more exposed."

Linnea stood, steady and firm. "This isn't about what's fastest. It's about what's best for all of us. We can backtrack to the proper crossing. One day, Celenneth. One day for her safety."

The words struck home. Celenneth stiffened. "Don't question my care for Brynja."

"Then show it," Linnea said quietly. "Make the choice that protects her — not just the one that pushes us on faster."

For a long moment the space between them was taut. Then Celenneth let out a sharp breath and her shoulders eased. "Fine. We backtrack. But if trouble finds us for the delay, we'd best be ready."

Linnea's expression softened. "We will be. Together."

⁂

As they neared Rivendell the very air changed — cooler, and stilled to something almost otherworldly. Golden-leaved trees lined the path, and moss softened their horses' hooves, and the wind brushed past like a whisper. Celenneth drew rein at the crest of a hill, and her breath caught as the valley opened below: the spires and arches of the Elven haven rising serene out of the mist. Linnea pulled up beside her, wide-eyed, and even Brynja leaned forward, pointing at the shimmering leaves. "It's beautiful," Linnea murmured.

As they descended, a welcoming party came out to meet them — Elves in silver and green, calm and ageless — and at their head walked Glorfindel, his golden hair bright as sunlight. "Celenneth of the Dúnedain," he said, warm and resonant. "Lord Elrond has been expecting you."

She dismounted, reverence and resolve mingled in her. "We've come far."

His gaze moved to Linnea and Brynja. "Your companions are welcome. Rivendell is a sanctuary for all."

Linnea slid down, gathering Brynja close, looking round her. "It feels safe," she said softly.

"Come," Glorfindel said. "You must be weary." And they were led in, and the weight of the road seemed to lift from them — though for Celenneth the shadow of the summons stayed, and whatever waited here, she would meet it with the two of them beside her.[^r-rivendell]

[^r-rivendell]: **Journey / Undertaking** — the family sets out from Edoras for Rivendell at Elrond's call (Freowyn remaining behind in Rohan). A cautious crossing chosen for Brynja's safety over speed; they reach Imladris and are received by Glorfindel.
