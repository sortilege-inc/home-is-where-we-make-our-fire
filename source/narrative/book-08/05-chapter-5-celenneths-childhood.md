# Chapter 5: Celenneth's Childhood

*Víressë 14, 2969*

The three of them sat close by the fire in the house they had taken for the season in the Angle, the hidden Dúnedain country north of Bree. Brynja lay curled in Celenneth's lap, small fingers tangled in her cloak, and Linnea rested against the arm of her chair, her eyes soft with thought.

"Celenneth," she said after a while, "you've never told me much of how you were raised. What was it like, with the rangers?"

Celenneth's gaze went distant, though a faint smile touched her. "It was different," she said, resting her chin on Brynja's dark curls. "The rangers aren't like families in towns. They don't speak of love. They don't say *I'm proud of you,* or *I care.* But I knew they did."

Brynja tilted her head up, frowning. "They didn't say *I love you,* Mama?"

"No, little one," Celenneth murmured, brushing a curl from her cheek. "But love isn't always in words. It's in how they saw me warm on the coldest nights, even giving up their own cloaks. In how they shared what little food they had without complaint. They didn't say it — but they showed it, every day."

"And the training?" Linnea asked.

Celenneth gave a low, knowing laugh. "Hard. Grueling. They didn't coddle me. The moment I could hold a bow, they saw I could use it well — tracking, hunting, climbing, fighting. It was all life or death to them. And in the wilds, it was." She paused, lost a moment in memory. "I was thirteen when they sent me on my first solo run. A map, a knife, two days' food. Find a marked place, and come back with proof I'd been there."

Linnea's brows lifted. "Alone? At that age?"

"They shadowed me without letting me know — I wasn't truly alone, though it felt it. Thirty miles through the wild. I was terrified. I made it, barely. And when I came back they only nodded, and told me to clean my boots."

Brynja's small fingers tightened in her cloak. "That sounds mean."

Celenneth smiled and kissed her forehead. "It might seem so. But they did it because they wanted me to survive. They needed me strong. And when I was in real danger, years later, those lessons kept me alive."

Linnea rested a warm hand on her arm. "They may never have said it. But it sounds like they loved you, in their way."

"They did," Celenneth agreed softly. "And I'll always be grateful. But I want Brynja to know she's loved every way there is — in words, in deeds, in all of it."

Brynja gazed up at her, wide and serious. "I know you love me, Mama."

Something in Celenneth's chest went full and tight, and she pulled her daughter close. "Good. Because I do. With all my heart."

The fire crackled into the quiet. Then Linnea spoke again, steady. "I was serious about what I said before. About finding the rangers who raised you — Brandor and Orlec. If they're still out there, we could look. We could go anywhere in the North that matters to you."

Celenneth blinked, surprised. "Linnea, you don't have to—"

"I want to," Linnea interrupted, firm but kind. "You've spent so much helping me reconnect with my past — with Bree, with my family. Let me do that for you."

Celenneth exhaled slowly, her gaze softening. "You'd truly do that?"

"Wherever you think they might be. Wherever feels like home to you. We'll go."

For the first time in what felt like an age, Celenneth let herself hope. "They moved often. But there are places they'd return to. Old camps near the Weather Hills. The forests east of Lake Evendim." Her mind was already running the routes, recalling half-forgotten trails; for so long she had thought that chapter of her life closed, those ties cut. But now there was a chance.

Linnea squeezed her hand. "Then we start there."

Celenneth drew a deep breath and nodded. "We'll find them." And for the first time in a long while the road ahead seemed clear — not merely a journey through far lands, but a journey toward belonging, and memory, and perhaps a home. Together.[^r-angle]
