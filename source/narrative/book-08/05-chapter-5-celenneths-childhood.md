# Chapter 5: Celenneth’s Childhood

*Book 8*

*Víressë 1, 2969*

The three of them sat close by the fire, its golden glow casting warm flickers across their faces, in the home they had found for the season in the hidden Dúnedain town of The Angle. Brynja lay curled up in Celenneth’s lap, her small fingers tangled in the folds of her mother’s cloak. Linnea rested against the arm of her chair, her eyes soft with curiosity and thought as she watched them.

After a moment, Linnea spoke, her voice low and thoughtful. “Celenneth... you’ve never told me much about how you were raised. What was it like, being with the rangers?”

Celenneth’s gaze turned distant, though her lips curved in a faint smile. “It was… different,” she said slowly, resting her chin lightly on Brynja’s dark curls. “The rangers aren’t like families in towns or villages. They don’t speak of love. They don’t say ‘I’m proud of you’ or ‘I care.’ But I knew they did.”

Brynja tilted her head up, brow furrowing in confusion. “They didn’t say ‘I love you,’ Mama?”

“No, little one,” Celenneth murmured, brushing a stray curl from Brynja’s cheek. “But love isn’t always in words. It’s in how they made sure I was warm on the coldest nights, even if it meant giving up their cloaks. It’s in how they shared what little food they had without complaint.” Her voice softened. “They didn’t say it... but they showed it every day.”

Linnea tilted her head, intrigued. “And the training?”

Celenneth let out a quiet, knowing laugh. “Hard. Grueling. They didn’t coddle me. The moment I could hold a bow, they made sure I could use it well. Tracking, hunting, climbing, fighting—it was all life or death to them. And in the wilds... it was.”

She paused, lost in memory. “I remember one of my first solo runs. I was thirteen. They handed me a map, a knife, and two days’ worth of food. Told me to find a marked place on the map... and come back with proof I’d been there.”

Linnea’s brows lifted. “Alone? At that age?”

Celenneth nodded, her expression unreadable. “They shadowed me without letting me know. I wasn’t truly alone—but it felt like I was.” Her voice grew quieter. “I was terrified at first. Thirty miles, through the wild. I made it. Barely. When I returned, they just nodded and told me to clean my boots.”

Brynja frowned, her small fingers tightening around Celenneth’s cloak. “That sounds mean.”

Celenneth smiled gently, pressing a kiss to her daughter’s forehead. “It might seem that way. But they did it because they wanted me to survive. They needed me to be strong. And when I was in real danger later... those lessons saved me.”

Linnea reached out, resting her hand on Celenneth’s arm. Her touch was warm, grounding. “They may not have said it, but it sounds like they loved you in their way.”

“They did,” Celenneth agreed softly. “And I’ll always be grateful... but I want Brynja to know she’s loved in every way. In words, in actions... in everything.”

Brynja gazed up at her mother, eyes wide and serious. “I know you love me, Mama.”

Celenneth’s heart swelled, and she pulled her daughter close. “Good... because I do. With all my heart.”

The fire crackled gently, filling the silence with warmth. Linnea shifted slightly, her expression thoughtful but resolute.

“I was serious about what I said before,” Linnea said quietly, her eyes steady on Celenneth. “About finding the rangers who raised you—Brandor and Orlec. If they’re still out there... we could look for them. We could go anywhere in the North that matters to you.”

Celenneth blinked, surprised. “Linnea... you don’t have to—”

“I *want* to,” Linnea interrupted, her voice firm but kind. “You’ve spent so much time helping me reconnect with my past—with Bree, with my family. I want to do that for you.”

Celenneth exhaled slowly, her gaze softening. “You’d really do that?”

Linnea smiled gently. “Wherever you think they might be... wherever feels like home to you, we’ll go.”

For the first time in what felt like forever, Celenneth allowed herself to hope. “They moved often... but there are places they might return to. Old camps near the Weather Hills... and the forests east of Lake Evendim.”

Her mind began racing, plotting routes, recalling half-forgotten trails. For so long, she’d thought that chapter of her life closed—those ties severed. But now... there was a chance.

Linnea squeezed her hand, her smile warm and steady. “Then we’ll start there.”

Celenneth drew in a deep breath, nodding slowly. “We’ll find them.”

And for the first time in a long time, the path ahead seemed clear—not just a journey through distant lands, but a journey toward belonging, memory, and perhaps... home. Together.
