# Chapter 2: The Glory of Rivendell

*Súlimë 20, 2969*

Under one of Rivendell's great arches Celenneth stood with Linnea and looked out over the valley, and even her composure gave way to awe. Twilight bathed it in gold and silver, the cascading falls shimmering like threads of starlight, the lanterns hung in the trees blending their warm glow with the haven's ethereal light. Brynja wriggled on Linnea's hip, reaching for the dancing lamps.

"I love its strangeness," Celenneth murmured, tipping her head back to the towering spires and their intricate carvings.

Linnea's voice was hushed. "It's like stepping into a song. So different from the Woodland Realm — so open, so full of light." She thought of Thalion's halls, dark and majestic beneath their canopy, cloaked in mystery; Rivendell seemed instead to embrace the stars, its beauty not hidden but sung aloud. An Elf passed, his robes flowing like water, his steps leaving no trace, and offered a serene smile; Linnea returned it, suddenly aware of her own dusty boots and frayed cloak. Another knelt to play a small silver harp, drawing a delighted squeal from Brynja, who reached to brush the strings and coax a faint sweet note. "They seem to belong to another age," Celenneth mused.

"They do," Linnea said. "Like they live outside of time." She sighed, shifting the child. "It's beautiful here. Peaceful. But I can't help feeling out of place."

Celenneth set a hand on her shoulder. "We're travelers here. But they welcome us."

⁂

The Hall of Fire lay in Rivendell's eternal twilight, and Elrond sat at the head of a low table, calm and commanding, the crackle of the hearth the only sound beneath the far whisper of the falls. Celenneth stood before him, composed despite the weight of the moment.

"Celenneth of the Dúnedain," Elrond began, steady and measured, "you have come far, and I thank you for it. But I bear tidings that will not be easy to hear."

She inclined her head, her hands tightening. "Speak plainly, Lord Elrond."

His gaze softened even as his tone carried its gravity. "Balin, son of Fundin, has fallen. He perished in Moria, seeking to reclaim its halls. Darkness rose against him and his kin, and though they fought valiantly, they were overwhelmed."

The words struck her with a quiet force. She drew a sharp breath, her jaw setting against the loss. "Balin was no fool," she said, low but steady. "He knew the risk. But he was determined. He believed Moria could be restored."

"He spoke of you with trust and respect," Elrond said. "Gandalf sent you to him for good reason. He valued your wisdom, and your strength."

Her throat tightened on the memories — Balin's laughter, his unbending spirit, the way he had spoken of his dreams for Khazad-dûm. "He was a good friend," she said quietly. "And a better leader than most credited."

An Elf stepped forward bearing a long cloth-wrapped bundle, and Elrond gestured to it. "There is more. The mithril Balin once entrusted to us has been wrought into armor — a gift in his memory, and a token of gratitude for your service." The cloth fell away to reveal a breastplate of gleaming mithril, its surface etched with swirling designs that caught the light like water under moonlight. Celenneth's breath caught as she stepped near.

"Light, and yet strong," Elrond said. "A protection worthy of one who walks in shadow to guard the light."

She reached out and brushed the cool metal, and for a moment the grief in her eased, replaced by a fierce resolve. "I will wear it," she said. "And honor Balin with every step."

Elrond inclined his head. "Let it serve you well, Celenneth of the Dúnedain. The road ahead will test you — but you are not alone."[^r-mithril]

⁂

Later, under an ancient elm by the murmuring streams, Linnea sat with Brynja asleep against her chest, and a little way off Celenneth stood speaking with one of Elrond's counselors, her bearing easy and sure. Watching her, Linnea felt a realization settle heavy in her: Celenneth was more than a ranger. Here, among Elves who treated her with quiet reverence, it showed plainly — she was a protector whose deeds rippled far beyond their small family.

When Celenneth returned, Linnea hesitated before she spoke. "I didn't understand until now how much others look to you. How much you carry."

Celenneth's brows rose, a faint smile touching her lips. "I do what needs doing. Like anyone."

"No," Linnea said, quiet but firm. "It's more than that. And I'm starting to see — there'll be times we can't hold you back. Times we'll have to let you go."

Celenneth crouched beside her. "You've never held me back, Linnea. You gave me a reason to come home." She glanced toward the valley where the stars were kindling over Rivendell's spires. "And you've made me see something. We're nearer Bree now than we've been in years. If we're ever to visit, it may as well be now."

Linnea blinked. "Bree? After everything Elrond called you here for?"

Celenneth smiled gently. "The road will always call. But Brynja won't always be this small. And you've given me more than a duty — you've given me a home. Besides, what Elrond needed of me was mostly what I already knew; whatever comes next, he can summon others for it."

Linnea's gaze fell to Brynja's small hand curled against her, then back to Celenneth. "If we go, it's not only for Brynja. It's for us. For finding something solid."

Celenneth's hand covered hers. "Then let's make it solid. Together."

[^r-mithril]: **Reward — Mithril armor; and the fall of Balin.** Elrond bears grievous news: Balin's colony in Moria has fallen. In Balin's memory and thanks for Celenneth's service, the dwarf's entrusted mithril has been forged into a light, strong breastplate — a Marvellous protection she vows to wear in his honor.
