# Chapter 1: The Watchtower

*Súlimë 3, 2970*

Celenneth led the company through the rugged eastern hills, her senses tuned to every shift in the land — wind-tossed leaves, the far cry of a hunting hawk, the earthy smell of thawing soil against lingering frost. Damrod and Hallas kept pace without complaint, their vigilance matching hers; Linnea followed close, her eyes ever moving. By dusk the old watchtower marked on her maps came into view, its crumbling upper walls standing like broken teeth against the dimming sky. Celenneth signed a halt, and they led the horses to a sheltered hollow.

"We observe from here," she said. "Damrod, Hallas, take the flanks. Linnea, keep watch." A flicker of light showed in one of the tower's lower windows, and she moved ahead — until her boot struck loose masonry and sent it clattering down the incline. She froze. The old door creaked open, and a hooded figure emerged with a shielded lantern; while it moved toward the noise, Celenneth slipped through the door left ajar, sword drawn. Outside, Damrod and Hallas closed on the figure from either side like shadows made flesh; Damrod struck the blade from its grasp, Hallas drove it back against the cold stone, and they had their captive.

Inside, Celenneth found a brazier's struggling glow and, beyond an archway, worn steps descending into dark. At their foot a Númenórean ward pulsed faint in the wall — long faded, but not dead. She touched it, whispering a phrase of warding drawn from her studies of the old Adûnaic tongue and what had served in the frozen ruin of the north, and the rune flared silver, and for an instant she felt its purpose whole: a seal meant to confine something powerful below. But the enchantment was cracked, and something down there had already begun to stir.[^r-ward]

Hallas joined her, and together they pressed deeper. The corridor descended steep and cold, and the cursed Fangorn blade at her hip — bound in stained cloth — throbbed with a subtle hunger she tried to name imagination. A sickly red glow appeared ahead, pulsing in time with a muffled, alien chanting, and easing to the half-open stone door she looked into a vaulted chamber: three robed figures around an altar bathed in crimson, and on it a bound captive, breathing shallow under coils of shadowy mist. Celenneth met Hallas's eyes. "We end this now — quiet and fast."

She drew, breathed, and loosed, and her arrow took the nearest cultist through with a muffled crack; he dropped in silence. The other two whirled, hands sparking with malignant fire, and her second shaft skittered off the altar-stone — but Hallas burst forward, and his sword opened the second cultist's torso, and his chant died with him. The last spat a final invocation at the captive, the chamber shuddering with corrupt power — and Celenneth's next arrow buried in his chest, cutting the words short, and Hallas's blade finished it. The ritual undone, the altar still glowed with erratic, unstable pulses. She pressed her palms to its runes and forced her mind through half-remembered Númenórean formulas, Hallas's steadying hand and his own scraps of lore guiding her, and together they drained the crimson light until only a faint silver glow remained, and the shadowy bonds around the captive shattered.[^r-ritual]

The freed woman was young, barely past twenty, pallid and bruised, her ranger's gear — runed bracers, a wood-and-bone pendant — hinting at a hard road. Celenneth knelt and took her cold hands and draped her own cloak round the trembling shoulders. "It's over. You're safe now. You're free." While Hallas searched the fallen cultists — turning up a foul rune-blade, a scroll-case of Black Speech, and an obsidian sigil-stone still crackling faintly — Celenneth coaxed the woman toward speech. "Morwenna," she managed at last. "I came from the North, seeking the Forgotten Watchers — but they found me first." The effort drained her, and she leaned weak against Celenneth as they carried her up into the night air.

Outside, by Damrod and Linnea's small fire, the bound captive stiffened at the sight of Morwenna, fury barely held. Linnea laid out the recovered relics in the firelight and pulled the gag free. "These — what purpose did they serve? What were you doing down there? Who do you serve?" The man sneered, then spat words like venom. "The Watchers stir, you blind fools. You've only delayed their rise, not stopped it. The bond endures. The Shadow waits." He laughed, hollow and brittle. Morwenna shivered, and forced out the rest of it: "The Watchers — sleeper agents of Morgoth, if the old stories hold. Ancient guardians, or fiends, meant to wake at some dark summons. Most thought them myth. But if they've stirred, someone is calling them."

Damrod's jaw set. "If he lives, he's a threat. He'll kill again." Hallas shook his head — "he might still be useful; fear loosens tongues, and we know so little" — and Celenneth weighed the burden between them. "I won't kill a bound and helpless man, however dark his deeds," she said at last. "We learn what we can. At dawn we search the tower, take what's needed, and set him free. His life isn't ours to judge." Damrod gave a reluctant nod; Linnea met her eyes in silent agreement; and they settled into an uneasy rest, the captive tied to a stout pine root, Morwenna dozing fitfully against Celenneth's shoulder, the watches shared beneath a cold and starlit sky.[^r-morwenna]
