The atmosphere between them shifted, the dusty silence charged by an unspoken current. They ascended a narrow, spiraling staircase. At the highest accessible level, they found what once might have been an observatory. Part of its roof was gone, exposing them to the slate-gray sky. Celenneth wandered among shattered stones, her attention snagged by old carvings half-eaten by weather and time. She could sense the lingering wards etched into the structure, but they told her little in their dormant state. Meanwhile, Damrod’s sharp eyes caught something else—a loose stone in a cracked pedestal.

He carefully worked the mechanism, a soft click sounding as a hidden compartment opened. He studied the journal’s faded script, frowning at symbols he couldn’t fully decipher. With a quiet oath, he tucked it away, determined to unravel its meaning later. The silence pressed around them like a held breath—unbroken, yet listening.

When they turned back toward each other, something in the room shifted. The runes, once dulled by centuries of neglect, flared suddenly with a silvery glow. Maybe it was the items they’d uncovered, or the honesty they had shared. Perhaps it was their resolve, their emotions stirring old powers once hidden in these stones. The dark whispers that lurked in the cracks and corners of the tower seemed to shrink back, subdued by this unexpected surge of clarity and strength.

For a long moment, Celenneth and Damrod stood close, breathing quietly in the renewed hush. The tower felt less like a tomb and more like a witness—the ancient walls calming, as if relieved by their presence. Something intangible yet potent had passed between them, stabilizing not only old magic but the fragile trust growing between two warriors who knew duty’s weight all too well.

Celenneth started back down the stairs. “Meet me at the end of the hallway, I want to inspect the rune there.” Damrod grunted an affirmative response.
