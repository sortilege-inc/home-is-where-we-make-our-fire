# Chapter 3: On the Road

*Book 11*

*Súlimë 5, 2970*

Morning found them gathered around a broad, flat stone, the rising sun softening the harsh lines of the tower’s silhouette behind them. Celenneth unrolled the map fragment carefully, pressing it flat with one hand while the other traced faded paths and cryptic markings. Nearby, Damrod placed the old ranger’s journal beside it, a frustrated crease pinching his brow. He’d spent part of the early dawn turning its pages in vain attempts to decipher the coded entries, but the meaning still eluded him. Hallas and Linnea knelt opposite each other, studying the dark relics—relics that still seemed to hum faintly with severed power.

Morwenna, though still pale, appeared more present than the night before. Wrapped in a borrowed cloak, she leaned forward, her voice low and cautious as she recounted what she remembered of her capture and quest. She spoke of half-ruined outposts and abandoned watchtowers hidden deep in the east, of rumors that these places might be tied to the awakening of the so-called Forgotten Watchers. Her memories were fragmented but compelling, each snippet of information weaving into the puzzle Celenneth and Damrod hoped to solve.

As they listened, the group weighed their next move. The eastern hills were calling—there, the scattered clues might align. They agreed to follow that route, leaving the crumbling tower and its grim secrets behind. With the decision made, they broke camp. Morwenna settled behind Hallas on his sturdy bay, her grip tentative but steady. Damrod and Celenneth readied their mounts, and Linnea checked their saddle-straps with the careful efficiency of the Rohirrim. Before long, the small company rode out, the tower shrinking behind them until it was just another broken shape on the horizon.

After a short while, Celenneth guided her horse closer to Linnea’s, letting the others drift ahead so they’d be out of easy earshot. The landscape rolled gently beneath them—low, forested hills dotted with early-spring buds, a hint of green returning to the world. The scent of damp earth and budding leaves drifted in the wind. They rode mostly in silence, content in each other’s presence, until Linnea caught Celenneth’s eye and arched a brow, inviting whatever had been on her mind.

Celenneth cleared her throat, voice soft enough that only Linnea could hear. “You asked me once about the possibility of... motherhood.” Her gaze flickered to where Damrod rode ahead, broad shoulders set in a familiar, confident posture. She lowered her voice further. “About whether we might try, if the opportunity arose. Well...” She paused, a faint blush warming her cheeks. “I told you what happened with Damrod in the tower. We... connected, in a way I hadn’t planned.”

Linnea’s eyes danced with curiosity and warmth, though she kept her tone even. “I hoped you’d tell me more.” she leaned in, a teasing smile on her lips.

Celenneth nodded, exhaling slowly. She described the intensity of that morning, the feeling of being swept into something powerful and elemental, how it surprised her as much as it thrilled her. Linnea listened with genuine interest, her expression thoughtful rather than jealous. When Celenneth mentioned the idea of having a child—something that had always been a distant notion, too tangled in duty and danger to feel real—Linnea’s face softened further.

“So you’re excited by the idea?” Linnea asked quietly.

Celenneth hesitated, then laughed softly. “It’s strange. I am. I never thought I would be, not with everything else going on, but there it is.”

Linnea reached over to place a reassuring hand atop Celenneth’s glove. “If this is something you want, keep going,” she said gently. “Damrod, Hallas... who knows what the road ahead holds? Maybe fortune will favor us in unexpected ways. I don’t desire men that way—my heart and body are yours—but that doesn’t mean I don’t want to see you happy, fulfilled.” Her grin turned playful. “And when you do find another moment to share with Damrod or Hallas, I’d like to be there. I’d like to watch.”

Celenneth’s cheeks heated, but a pleased smile curved her lips. “You would?” she asked, amused and touched all at once.

Linnea chuckled, a mischievous light in her eyes. “Absolutely. Consider it my way of encouraging good fortune,” she teased. Then her gaze grew earnest. “We’ve always trusted each other. I trust your heart—if this is a step you want to take, I’ll stand with you.”

They rode on, the quiet hills stretching ahead, birdsong drifting in the crisp air. The conversation wound into silence for a bit as Celenneth turned Linnea’s words over in her mind. The idea that she could embrace this desire, that Linnea would support her, filled her with a quiet thrill. She’d been a ranger and a warrior for so long, used to battles and grim tasks. Now, she faced a different kind of future—one woven from love and longing, hope and possibility.

Ahead, Hallas glanced back, perhaps curious why they lagged behind. Damrod, too, slowed his mount slightly, waiting for them to catch up. Celenneth and Linnea exchanged a knowing smile, urging their horses forward at a leisurely pace. Amid looming danger and uncertain horizons, there was still room for dreams—and the promise of new life, in all its forms.

The fire’s soft glow cast amber light across the camp, flickering like distant stars as the wind whispered through the hills. The quiet of the night stretched on, unbroken save for the occasional pop of wood burning low in the flames. Linnea sat on watch, her gaze drifting lazily to the heavens above—those vast, unknowable constellations that had guided them for weeks—but tonight, her mind refused to settle.

A different longing stirred in her chest, one far more immediate and far more familiar.

Her gaze slid from the fire to Celenneth, her lover’s sleeping form half-hidden beneath a weathered cloak. Strands of auburn hair slipped free from the hood, catching the faint light and glinting like burnished copper. Linnea’s heart quickened as her eyes lingered on Celenneth's peaceful face, the sharp angles softened in sleep. Even in rest, she looked so capable, so strong—a woman who carried burdens no one else could. Something fierce swelled within Linnea then, as undeniable as it was tender. She needed Celenneth. Needed her warmth. Needed to pull her close, to remind herself that in the vastness of their journey, some things were still wholly theirs.

Without a sound, Linnea moved to her knees and slipped through the shadows toward Celenneth’s bedroll. She hesitated only a moment, the cool night air wrapping around her, before reaching down. Her fingers, roughened by travel and bowstring alike, brushed over Celenneth’s shoulder—tracing the familiar line of her scarred arm, where battle had once marked her but could not break her. Her touch lingered, firm yet gentle.

Celenneth stirred, her sharp instincts rousing her immediately. Bleary-eyed but ready, her hand twitched toward the blade at her side until her vision focused on Linnea’s face hovering above her—dark eyes glimmering in the firelight, lips curled into the faintest smile. Celenneth relaxed, her body sinking back into the bedroll as a wry grin tugged at the corner of her mouth.

“Couldn’t sleep?” Celenneth murmured, voice husky and low, still heavy with drowsiness. The warmth of her tone reached through Linnea like a slow, spreading fire.

Linnea tilted her head, eyes dark with intent. “Didn’t want to,” she whispered, the words soft but deliberate as her fingers brushed an errant lock of hair from Celenneth’s face. Her thumb traced the edge of her jaw, lingering for just a moment longer than needed.

Celenneth raised a brow, but before she could quip in reply, Linnea leaned in, closing the space between them. Her lips pressed softly to Celenneth’s—tentative at first, then firm, lingering. It wasn’t rushed. It was reverent, almost savoring, as though Linnea were tasting something she’d longed for all night. Her breath caught as Celenneth’s arms rose, strong and sure, wrapping around her waist and pulling her down fully into the bedroll.

Linnea gasped softly against Celenneth’s mouth, their lips parting, breath mingling as they drank each other in. The world outside their fire dimmed, leaving only this—a closeness steeped in trust, in hunger, in a love that burned hotter than the flames beside them.

Linnea shifted, straddling Celenneth’s hips, the slow drag of her body against her lover’s drawing a soft groan from the woman below. “Linnea,” Celenneth breathed, her voice cracking like kindling as Linnea dipped down again, capturing her lips in a kiss deeper than the first—searing and needful. The taste of her sent something molten pooling low in Linnea’s belly. She pulled back only to trail her lips down Celenneth’s jaw, grazing the sensitive skin there, her tongue flicking just enough to make Celenneth suck in a sharp breath.

Clothing loosened in practiced silence. Linnea’s hands moved with care, fingers brushing scars and skin alike—memorizing, rediscovering, mapping her all over again as though the night had granted them new bodies to learn. Celenneth’s cloak slipped away, leaving her bare beneath Linnea, her skin glowing gold where the firelight touched it. Linnea stared down at her—at the strong, capable woman who had fought shadows and storms and still met every challenge with an unshakable resolve. In this moment, though, her guard was down, her body yielding to Linnea’s touch, her green eyes dark and hazy with desire.

“Come here,” Celenneth murmured, the command softened by a smile that reached her eyes.

Linnea obeyed, pressing her body flush against Celenneth’s as their mouths found each other again. The friction of bare skin sliding together sent sharp jolts of pleasure through Linnea, her pulse roaring in her ears, drowning out everything but Celenneth’s ragged breathing and the quiet, breathy sounds she made when Linnea touched her just so. Linnea moved slow at first, savoring every tremor she drew from Celenneth, every gasped whisper of her name.

And then she deepened her touch, her fingers working in slow, deliberate circles, knowing exactly how to unravel Celenneth piece by piece. Celenneth’s head fell back, her lips parting on a gasp that turned quickly into a moan, her body arching up into Linnea’s like a bow drawn taut. Linnea’s mouth found her throat, kissing and biting softly as she worked her into a slow, inevitable crescendo.

Unseen, across the camp, Hallas stirred, which Damrod and Morwenna continued to slumber. His instincts tugged him gently from sleep, his eyes snapping open to the subtle rustle of movement beyond the dying fire. He stilled immediately, his keen gaze locking on the shadowed forms just past the flames.

Celenneth and Linnea—wrapped together in the flickering glow—moved with an intimacy that spoke of trust as much as desire. The way Linnea touched Celenneth, kissed her, cradled her as though she were something precious and fragile, stole the breath from Hallas’s chest. He should have looked away. Should have turned over and buried himself back into the false comfort of sleep. But he couldn’t.

He watched silently, his breathing shallow, his sharp features softened by the way the firelight played across them. There was no malice in his gaze, only longing—something deep and unspoken that he’d buried for longer than he cared to admit.

Celenneth’s quiet moan carried across the camp, and Hallas’s chest tightened. Linnea’s movements slowed, her lips brushing against Celenneth’s ear, whispering something too soft to hear. Whatever she said drew a shuddering breath from Celenneth as she came undone, her body taut beneath Linnea’s, her face a portrait of pure surrender as her climax swept through her.

Linnea watched her, reverent, brushing sweat-damp hair back from her lover’s face as she whispered something soft—a murmur that only Celenneth could hear. The two of them curled into one another afterward, their limbs entwined, faces only inches apart as the fire burned low and the shadows grew long.

Hallas swallowed hard, his throat dry as he rolled carefully onto his back. He stared at the stars above, his expression unreadable. Yet something in his gaze flickered—soft and wistful, like the glow of embers hidden beneath ash.
