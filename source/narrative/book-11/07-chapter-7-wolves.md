# Chapter 7: Wolves

*Víressë 1, 2970*

After the festival they pressed southward, the hills softening under a gentler sky, spring warmth seeping into the land. Celenneth and Linnea rode side by side, sharing easy smiles; Damrod and Hallas kept their companionable silence, a steady rhythm at their backs. Late one afternoon they made camp beside a forgotten stone cairn, and as dusk fell a hush settled over the woodland — too pressed, as though the trees held their breath. Hallas caught movement at the clearing's edge. They were not alone.

The silence broke with a low, guttural growl, and out of the gloom came creatures shaped like wolves but warped by a darker power — eyes a feverish red, fur wavering half-there and half-not, like living silhouettes peeled from midnight. No natural beasts, but things of shadow and corruption. Celenneth and Linnea loosed at once; Celenneth's shaft vanished into the dark, but Linnea's punched into a wolf's shoulder, and it hissed like escaping steam and staggered — hurt, then, whatever it was.

The pack lunged with startling speed, flickering in and out of the real as they came. Hallas drove his spear low beneath one's ribs, and for a moment its shadowy hide thinned to reveal something black and oily beneath; Damrod's blade left a ragged gash that seeped darkness rather than blood; and Linnea's arrow, aimed true for a vital place, sank deep, and the beast lost all coherence and dissolved into a swirl of black mist. They fought in a storm of snarls and flickering shapes, the wolves' teeth snapping through empty air where flesh had been a moment before — but steel and arrow and the four of them together were enough, and one by one the creatures were driven apart into fading dark, until the last melted silently into the forest gloom.[^r-wolves]

In the silence after, their breath loud in their own ears, Damrod and Hallas exchanged a grave look and drew a little apart to speak low. These wolves had not come by chance; there was a source, a blight hidden beyond these hills, and Hallas gestured eastward.

Over a spare breakfast the next morning, Damrod said it plainly. "Hallas and I will track this corruption east. It's best we don't ignore it — there may be worse ahead." Hallas added, "You can come, if you wish. But we won't turn aside, and it may lead us far afield."

Celenneth and Linnea traded a look. They had come so far — and home, the Angle, called them now more than ever, and Brynja with it. "No," Celenneth said gently. "Our road leads south." Linnea touched her arm in agreement. "We'd only slow you. Go, find what you must. We'll hope for your safe return." There was a moment of understanding that needed no words. Damrod inclined his head, a rare softness in his eyes; Hallas clasped Celenneth's forearm, warrior to warrior; and then they embraced, all four, before the two men turned east into the hush beneath the canopy and were swallowed by the shifting morning light. Celenneth and Linnea mounted and angled south, toward gentler valleys and home, and the forest sighed behind them as their hoofbeats faded into silence.[^r-part]

[^r-wolves]: **Combat** — shadow-warped wolves (things of corruption, flickering between the real and the unreal) ambush the camp; the four destroy them, learning they can be hurt by steel and arrow aimed true. A sign of a deeper blight to the east.
[^r-part]: **Fellowship** — Damrod and Hallas turn east to hunt the corruption to its source; Celenneth and Linnea, drawn home to Brynja and the Angle, part from them with an embrace and the hope of reunion.
