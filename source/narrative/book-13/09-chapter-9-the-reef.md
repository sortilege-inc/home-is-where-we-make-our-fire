# Chapter 9: The Reef

*Book 13*

Celenneth stood at the rail of the Eärlindë, watching the Grey Havens recede behind them as the ship cut into open waters. She had often roamed wild hills and dark forests, yet the ocean’s vastness felt new and unsettling. Ahead lay three or more weeks of travel—long days of salt wind and rolling waves.

At first, the sailing was calm enough to let her relax. She saw Brynja perched along the deck’s edge with Linnea nearby, and Hallas drowsing in a sling across Celenneth’s chest. The child’s soft breathing provided a gentle counterpoint to the crew’s movements. Calion, the captain, kept a watchful eye on the horizon, while Ithildur, the navigator marked with dark tattoos, consulted his charts. All seemed in order, at least for a time.

One morning, the weather shifted quickly, dark clouds marching across the sky. The waves grew choppy, rocking the Eärlindë more fiercely with each hour. Celenneth tried to brace herself against the rail, swallowing down any queasiness. She caught sight of Linnea holding Brynja close, the little girl’s face pale with fear, and Eryndil drifting from person to person, offering calm words and water.

Celenneth’s own stomach knotted each time the ship lurched upward and plunged down again. She reminded herself she had endured worse on land—battling orcs or facing bitter storms in the Frost Maw—but there was no ground here, only undulating water that obeyed no mortal will. Eventually, the seas calmed, leaving Celenneth shaken but grateful as she joined Linnea in reassuring Brynja that the worst had passed.

Not two days later, a new obstacle appeared. Ithildur noticed an ominous pattern in the water—a reef stretching under the surface, barely visible except for the way waves broke unexpectedly. The crew sprang into a tense dance of orders and quick movements. Calion slowed the ship, urging caution. Eryndil positioned herself near the bow, her sharp eyes scanning for hidden rocks, while Lindarë, the quartermaster, coordinated the deckhands to adjust sails and lines in an effort to weave around the reefs.

Celenneth, determined to help, handed Hallas to Linnea and rushed forward to manage extra ropes and lines alongside the Elves. She focused on each shouted command, sweat beading at her temple. For a time, it seemed they might pass the reef without harm—until the wind changed unpredictably, and the ship jolted against a submerged rock. A scraping noise ripped through the air, the hull shuddering beneath Celenneth’s boots. She nearly lost her footing, gripping the rail hard as voices rose in alarm around her.

After a moment of panicked scrambling, the Eärlindë steadied, but the damage was evident. Calion and Ithildur both wore grim expressions as they confirmed the hull had taken significant damage. Mallor, the carpenter, disappeared below deck, returning with the troubled assessment: the needed repairs could not be safely made out on open water.

Calion called the crew together, standing at the center of the deck with quiet resolve. He explained they would need to find a harbor to address the hull’s damage. Though the plan involved a detour that would cost them time, there was no choice if they wished to sail on without risking a dangerous leak. Celenneth exhaled, aware of Linnea’s steadying presence at her side. She could see the worry in Brynja’s eyes, the confusion in Hallas’s small whimper, and the resolve in Eryndil’s calm face.

When the ship turned course, Celenneth felt a pang of frustration. Every day spent detouring meant slower progress toward the distant island that Círdan had asked them to find. At last, after a few tense days, land appeared at the horizon—rocky cliffs forming a natural bay, safe enough for the Eärlindë to anchor without fear of deeper hull damage. Celenneth exhaled in relief. She spotted Linnea holding Brynja aloft so the girl could see the coastline, and Hallas roused from a nap, gazing around with wide eyes.
