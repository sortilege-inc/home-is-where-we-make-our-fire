# Chapter 8: Brynja Meets the Crew

*Book 13*

Quietly, Brynja made her way around the belowdecks. No one seemed to mind her questions—most gave her a kind smile, amused at the eager child who wanted to know everyone’s story.

### Calion (Captain)

Brynja first approached a tall Elf studying a star chart near the helm. His lined face bore signs of ancient wisdom, and faint scars hinted at hard-won experience.

“Are you Calion?” she asked, tilting her head.Calion turned, revealing gentle eyes that somehow shone like distant stars. “I am,” he said, rolling up the chart. “And you must be Brynja. It’s an honor to have you aboard.”He tapped the charts under one arm. “I’m a starseer—some say the stars speak to me more than any compass. They’ve guided me through many a storm, and I’ve lived to tell the tales.”Brynja grinned. “So you can see the future in the stars?”Calion chuckled. “Not precisely. But sometimes, they offer hints—especially if you’ve traveled as long as I have.”
She noticed a battered sword at his hip and guessed he had no shortage of stories. But an easygoing calm surrounded him, making her feel instantly secure.

### Ithildur (Navigator)

At the table next to Calion’s charts, Brynja found a dark-haired elf with intricate tattoos coiling down his arms. Each design looked frightening and fierce: dragons, beasts, and strange symbols. He glanced up, and she caught a flicker of something unsettling in his eyes—but not unkind.

“You’re Ithildur?” she asked in a smaller voice, unsure.He inclined his head, a half-smile forming. “Aye. I’m the navigator. I work closely with Captain Calion. I suppose you notice my markings?”Brynja nodded, curious and a little nervous. “They’re… big. And scary. But kind of amazing.”Isildur ran a hand over a coiled beast on his forearm. “They mark foes I’ve faced—foes touched by shadow, as I once was. But fear not,” he added, voice dropping to a softer tone, “the shadows I know cannot truly withstand the light.”
She felt a tinge of sadness in his voice, but he gave her a gentle nod of encouragement, and she bobbed her head in return before moving on.

### Lindarë (Quartermaster)

Past the helm, Brynja nearly bumped into an elf woman lifting a heavy crate of supplies as though it weighed no more than a feather. Muscles bunched beneath her tunic, and she secured the crate with a single hand.

“Whoa,” Brynja whispered in awe. “You’re really strong.”The Elf smiled, setting the crate down. “Call me Lindarë. Quartermaster of the Eärlindë. Someone’s got to track every rope, nail, and biscuit aboard—and haul them around too.”Brynja’s eyes darted to a grappling hook hanging at Lundari’s belt. “You fight with that?” she asked, mouth agape.Lindarë shrugged with a half-grin. “In close quarters, yes. Surprises many a foe. Mind you, I keep the inventory ledger too—so I’d better not lose my own gear.”She let out a laugh that Brynja found strangely comforting, like a friendly giant ready to protect them if trouble arose.

### Nithrandir (Surgeon)

Not far off, an elderly elf with honey-blond-approaching-white hair lounged against a railing, swirling a small flask in his hand. He offered Brynja a dramatic bow when she approached.

“Good day, little lady,” he said, grin full of charm. “I’m the ship’s surgeon, and inveterate gossip, Nithrandir.”Brynja giggled at his exaggerated manner. “Surgeon? For broken bones??”Nithrandir nodded, tucking the flask away. “I can set bones, stitch wounds, and I have dabbled in the apothecary arts. A little bit of skill, a gentle word—both go a long way.” He winked. “And not to worry, Brynja, I keep the flask for… emergencies.”Something about his confident posture and easy banter made Brynja’s shoulders relax. “I hope you don’t have to fix me,” she admitted.He tipped an invisible hat. “That makes two of us, little one.”

### Eärion (Boatswain)

Moving along, Brynja noticed a slim Elf man with numerous rings on each hand. Perched on his shoulder was a vivid green bird—the brightest Brynja had ever seen. It let out a soft trill.

“Hello!” Brynja said shyly. “What kind of bird is that?”The Elf smiled, his voice light. “Her name’s Tuila. She’s a dear companion who helps me keep watch on the rigging. I’m Eärion, the boatswain. I oversee ropes, sails, anything that keeps this ship afloat. Also in charge of discipline, but I tend to let Tuila squawk if someone’s lazy.”Tuila squawked on cue, making Brynja giggle. “She can spy for you?” Brynja asked.Eärion nodded, jingling his rings softly. “Some say she sees the small details we might miss. Don’t worry; she’s friendly enough—just don’t give her all your crackers.”

### Celebrin (Deckhand)

Brynja then spotted a lithe elf woman sitting on a barrel, puffing on a pipe. An earthy aroma wafted Brynja’s way—strong but not unpleasant.

“Who’re you?” Brynja asked, curiosity brimming.The Elf grinned so broadly that dimples appeared. “Name’s Celebrin, deckhand here. I can spin a rope or climb a mast faster than you can blink. Also, I can sing a tune that’d make a troll blush.” She let out a laugh, surprisingly loud for an Elf.Brynja blinked at her frankness. “You… smoke pipeweed?” she ventured.Celebrin winked. “I do indeed. Helps pass the time, plus I’ve got a bit of a… well, let’s call it a ‘colorful vocabulary’ that might shock your dear mother.”Brynja giggled, both intrigued and a little unsure. Celebrin just gave her a playful salute, then resumed puffing with a free-spirited grin.

### Galaniel (Cook)

Scents of freshly baked bread wafted from an open hatch where a plump elf man in an apron stirred something in a steaming pot. His voice boomed with warmth even before Brynja stepped into view.

“Ah, a little traveler!” he exclaimed, waving a wooden spoon. “I’m Galaniel, the cook. Fancy a taste?”Brynja stepped closer, inhaling a savory aroma that made her stomach rumble. “Yes, please!” she said eagerly.He chuckled, offering a small wooden cup of stew. “Give that a try. By the time we’re halfway across the sea, you’ll be singing my praises—or, at least, I’ll try not to put you to sleep with my stories.”“You tell stories?” she asked between bites, bright-eyed.He laughed so heartily the spoon bounced in his hand. “Certainly do, and I’ve a knack for getting everyone up and motivated—or lulled into a quick nap if that’s needed. Speaking of naps, I’m told I can doze off just about anywhere, mid-sentence even.”Brynja nearly choked on her stew from giggling so hard. She thanked him politely before dashing off again.

### Thalandir (Deckhand)

In a shady corner, an elf man with a securely-bound row of potted** **herbs and an eyepatch meticulously watered his seedlings. The sight reminded Brynja of a humble gardener rather than a seafarer.

“Hi,” she said quietly, tilting her head at his eyepatch. “Are you growing plants?”He paused in his watering, smiling gently. “I am. Does my patch surprise you? My name is Thalandir”Brynja nodded solemnly. “What are you growing?” She peered at the tiny buds in their pots.A soft chuckle escaped him. “Mostly hardy herbs. I try to keep something growing whenever I’m at sea. Reminds me that life always finds a way.”She thought that was beautiful—an odd, tender dream amid a rough, ocean-bound life.

### Voriel (Lookout)

High above, perched in the crow’s nest, Brynja glimpsed a figure scanning the horizon. Later, she bumped into the same Elf woman climbing swiftly down the mast. Short, wavy hair framed the Elf’s keen eyes, and she immediately flashed Brynja a grin that was half mischief, half greeting.

“I’m Voriel, the lookout,” she announced cheerily, dusting off her breeches. “Nothing escapes these eyes, day or night.”Brynja shivered with delight, imagining Voriel spotting hidden islands or looming sea monsters from up high. “You can see in the dark?” she asked in awe.Voriel’s grin widened. “Pretty well, yes. It’s why Calion—er, Captain Calion—trusts me in rough waters. But watch yourself,” she added in a conspiratorial whisper, “I might play a prank or two if the voyage gets dull.”Brynja giggled. “What kind of prank?”Voriel winked. “Wouldn’t you like to know?” She then bounded off, light as a cat, leaving Brynja imagining all sorts of mischief up in the crow’s nest.

### Mallor (Carpenter)

Across the deck, Brynja noticed an elf man scribbling something in a small journal, lips pressed into a thoughtful line. Nearby lay a set of carpentry tools—planes, chisels, saws. He glanced up from time to time to watch the rigging, as though listening to the ship’s heartbeat.

“What are those?” Brynja asked, stepping closer.The Elf looked up, revealing warm brown eyes. “I keep a journal of omens and runes, seeing hints of what may come. Not always certain, of course.” he said softly.She tilted her head, intrigued. “Like… you can see the future?”He offered a faint, humble shrug. “I see possibilities. Whether they come to pass is another matter. But rest easy, Brynja—I believe this voyage has blessings behind it. But mostly I do the carpentry, not the fortune-telling.”His calm sincerity soothed her. “Is that why you fix the ship, too?” she asked. “So you can guide the future in a way?”He cracked a gentle smile, flipping the journal closed. “In a sense, yes. Sometimes building or mending is the best fortune-telling you can do.”

### Elenmir (Sailmaker)

Brynja then passed by the foredeck, where a slender elf woman methodically inspected a partially folded sail. Her silvery hair shimmered, and though her face was quiet, an intense focus burned behind her eyes.

“Hello,” Brynja ventured carefully. “Did you make these?”She looked up, expression grave yet not unkind. “I did. My name is Elenmir. Mind the edges, child.” She nodded at the sharp sewing needles pinned to a cloth beside her. “These sails can’t afford flaws, especially in a storm.”Brynja swallowed, feeling a shiver at the seriousness in Elenmir’s tone. “You seem… strong,” she said timidly. “Mama says I can see who’s a fighter.”Elenmir’s lips twitched, not quite a smile. “I prefer not to fight, but if forced, I don’t hold back.” A certain fierceness flickered in her eyes, then vanished as quickly as it appeared. “Yet I’d rather be alone with my stitching than raging in a battle.”Brynja nodded, sensing a storm behind Elenmir’s reserved exterior. She respectfully left Elenmir to her work.

### Silivren (Deckhand)

Finally, Brynja spotted a tall elf woman practicing sword forms near the prow—two swords, actually, each etched with runes. Her movements were fluid and confident, suggesting a lethal skill.

“Wow,” Brynja breathed, unable to resist stepping closer. “You’re… good.”The Elf paused, lowering her blades. “Silivren, deckhand,” she said, wiping her brow. “And you must be Brynja, if I’m not mistaken.”Brynja nodded vigorously. “Those swords… are they special?”Silivren’s gaze slid over the runes, a hint of pride in her features. “They’re heirloom weapons—been in my family for generations. I’ve faced enough foes to know they’re more than mere steel.” A humble pride mingled with her quiet confidence. “I’ll keep them sheathed unless there’s real danger, though.”The child smiled shyly. “I hope we won’t need them.”Silivren gave a small chuckle. “I hope so, too.” Then she offered a nod of goodbye, turning back to her practice.

After meeting each crewmember, Brynja retreated to her favorite nook near the ship’s bow, her thoughts awhirl. She’d just discovered a whole new world of characters—each with their own gifts, quirks, and stories. In many ways, the Eärlindë felt less like a mere vessel and more like a roaming village of unusual friends.

Her parents found her there, swinging her legs, and Celenneth tousled her hair. “You’ve been busy,” Linnea teased, lifting Hallas slightly so Brynja could see him blink awake. “Making new friends?”

Brynja’s eyes shone with excitement. “Yes!” she exclaimed. “I’m so glad they’re all here!”
