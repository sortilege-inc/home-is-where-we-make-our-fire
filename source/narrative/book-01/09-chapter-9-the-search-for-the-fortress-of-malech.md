# Chapter 9: The Search for the Fortress of Malech

*Lótessë 17, 2965*

Balin's next want was plain: the Fortress of Malech, a strongpoint the orcs held in numbers, and worth the taking to loosen their grip on the deep. He gathered his hardest for it — Celenneth among a war band of a dozen and more, every one of them tempered by the halls behind them.[^r-band]

It would be a long road, and every league of it through orc-held ground. There would be no slipping down quiet passages this time; the way to Malech ran through the enemy's own country, and they went into it braced for the fight to find them before they found the fortress.

They travelled the way a war band travels in hostile dark — Ginar and Nar out ahead reading the ground, the archers loose in the middle, the rest close, torches shielded, voices down to nothing. It made for slow, taut going, the kind that wears a company more than hard marching ever does: every hall entered as though it might be held, every silence weighed and measured before a foot was set into it.

On the first day Fenja's eye earned its keep — a sheer rock-face that looked impassable and was not, if a dwarf knew where to set an iron. They spent hours at it, driving pitons and rigging rope, and hauled themselves up and over, and came out above the orc-thick passages they would otherwise have had to cut through: a hard climb traded for a harder battle, and counted well spent.[^r-climb]

The second day began as well — good time through the winding ways, a collapsed tunnel picked through rather than turned from — until a stone bridge betrayed them. They were halfway across a span over a deep dark gulf when orcs came on from both ends at once, a party closing before and another behind, and the bridge became a trap with the company caught in the jaws of it. There was no thought in it, only the doing: Hanar cut for the far side without a word, hewing a path through the orcs that held it; Ida's bolts snapped out of the middle to harry the nearest off their stride; Celenneth held the span itself, her sword and her body between the dwarves and the ones coming up behind, buying the crossing with every heartbeat she could steal. They came off the bridge in a frantic press and up a broken hill into a tunnel-mouth — and there, Ginar's quick hands, brought the loose slope down behind them in a small roaring avalanche that sealed the way and their pursuers with it. Won, and none of them lost. But the near thing rode out with them, the shadow of it heavy on the whole band.[^r-bridge]

The third day the strain of that told. They went slower, and Nar flagged, and no one blamed him for it; the deep did not care that they had won the bridge, and only offered more of itself — more dark, more stone, more distance. The fourth day brought them, late and worn, to a thing none of them expected: a small dwarven home carved into the rock off a forgotten passage, long ransacked, its shelves bare and its furniture overturned, a single bed and a sink and a little workbench gone to dust. And yet there was a homeliness to it even in the ruin — the shape of a life once lived down here in the quiet, a dwarf who had risen and worked and slept in this small room in the belly of the world. It put something back in the company to see it. It was, Celenneth thought, the very thing all of this was for.

On the fifth day they came to the Cave of Lights, where the walls glimmered with a cold luminescence that owed nothing to their torches, and high in the dark above it Celenneth marked an old dwarven lantern gone dull with the years — no common lamp, that, but a made thing, one that might be woken again with the mending of it. They noted where it hung, to come back for if ever the road allowed, and went on.

And the road ran out at Malech. The great stairs up to the fortress were watched, orc sentries set at their feet — but a careful circuit of the ground found a second stair, unguarded, winding up the far side, and they took it quiet, hearts high in their throats, eyes on the dark. At the top, the dark gave them what they had come all that long road to find: Uftak. An orc greater than any they had faced, ranged about with a gleaming hoard of polished weapons — swords and axes of dwarves and elves and men and orcs alike, taken and kept and hung like trophies against the day one more fool came climbing.

[^r-band]: The war band for Malech — Celenneth with Hanar, Dagrun, Ida, Ginar, Nar, Regin, and Frerin, Fenja, Yrr, Sefa, Lif, and Tora. A dozen and more of Balin's hardest.
[^r-climb]: **Travel** — Fenja finds a climbable rock-face (pitons and rope); a shortcut that skirts the orc-held passages and spares them a battle.
[^r-bridge]: **Combat / escape** — caught on a bridge between two orc parties; Hanar carves the way, Ida's crossbow harries, Celenneth holds the span. All across; the pursuit sealed off by a dropped avalanche. A touch of **Shadow** from the near thing.
