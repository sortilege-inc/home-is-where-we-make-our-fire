# Chapter 9: The Search for the Fortress of Malech

*Book 1*

*Lótessë 17, 2965*

Balin's next objective was clear: find the Fortress of Malech, an area of importance currently held by the orcs, and claim it. It was a strategic location, rumored to be a stronghold where the orcs gathered in large numbers. Balin's hope was to weaken their grip on Moria, and so he gathered his most capable fighters, forming a war band. Celenneth, along with Fenja, Yrr, Sefa, Frerin, Lif, Tora, Ginar, Ida, Nar, Regin, Hanar, and Dagrun, was chosen for this mission—a group that was strong, hardened by the many battles fought within these dark halls.

The war band was prepared for a long journey, armed with supplies and weapons. The path ahead would be fraught with danger; they would be traversing into the heart of orc-held territory, cutting through enemy lines. It was not a task for the faint-hearted.

On the first day, they moved swiftly, their footsteps silent against the stone floor. Fenja, always observant, spotted what appeared to be a rocky face—a sheer cliff that seemed impassable. Upon closer inspection, she realized it was navigable with the right tools. The group worked together, setting up pitons and ropes, scaling the rock. Though it took time, it ultimately allowed them to shave several miles off their journey and avoid orc-infested areas. They managed to travel eight miles that day, cutting another four miles from their trip in the process.

The second day began well, the group making phenomenal time through the winding passages. They found a collapsed tunnel, and rather than turning back, they decided to navigate through the rubble, again managing to cut time from their journey. Spirits were high as they crossed an old stone bridge, their progress uninterrupted—until they found themselves caught between two approaching parties of orcs, converging on them from opposite directions.

Hanar’s eyes narrowed, and without hesitation, he moved to cut a path forward. Ida harried the orcs with her crossbow, her bolts finding their marks with deadly precision, while Celenneth stood her ground, holding the orcs at bay. They focused on escape, pushing hard in the direction they had been traveling, rushing to get off the bridge before the orcs could block both ends. It was a tense, frantic scramble, but they managed to get all the dwarves across, hurrying up a hill and into the relative safety of another tunnel. The group worked quickly to knock loose rocks from the hill, creating a small avalanche that blocked the path behind them, cutting them off from their pursuers. It was a hard-won victory, but they succeeded, though the shadow of the encounter left its mark.

On the third day, the journey continued, though the strain of the previous days was beginning to show. Nar, one of their scouts, was visibly fatigued, his steps slower than usual. They managed to travel another six miles, but the progress was slower, the weight of their task heavy on their shoulders.

By the fourth day, they had covered another ten miles, their determination unwavering. It was late in the day when they stumbled upon a small dwarven domicile—a long-abandoned home carved into the rock. It had been ransacked, the shelves bare, the furniture overturned, but there was a strange sense of homeliness to it. A single bed, a sink, a small workspace—reminders of what life could be like, even in the depths of Moria. The sight gave the dwarves a renewed sense of purpose, a glimpse of what they were fighting for.

On the fifth day, they pressed on, their path taking them to a place known as the Cave of Lights. The walls glimmered with a strange luminescence, and far above, Celenneth spotted an old dwarven lantern, its surface dulled with age. She could tell it was no ordinary lantern—it seemed as though it could be magically relit, perhaps repaired. They made note of it, deciding to return if time allowed.

The wide stairs leading up to the Fortress of Malech were guarded, orc sentries stationed at the base. But with careful reconnaissance, they found a second staircase, one that was unguarded and winding its way up the opposite side. Moving quietly, they ascended the hidden path, their eyes scanning the darkness for any sign of danger.

At the top, they found themselves presented with a massive orc, larger than any they had seen before. He was surrounded by gleaming, polished weapons—swords and axes of dwarves, elves, men, and orcs alike; Uftak.
