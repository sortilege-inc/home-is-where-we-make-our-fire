# Chapter 8: New Frontiers / Base Camp

*Víressë 28, 2965*

With the orcs thrown back and her wounds half-mended, the days filled with the work of holding a camp in a dead kingdom — shoring the defences, standing the watches, taking what quiet the dark allowed. It was in one such lull that Balin came to her, Hanar at his side.

"We need water untainted by orc-filth," Balin said, direct as ever. "Deeper in. Somewhere we can make a lasting base. I'm sending you two and your crew to find it. Lif and Tora — two you brought up off the ledge — want to go with you. Expect to cut your way through."

She traded a look with Hanar, who gave his resolute nod. "We'll get it done."

They went out nine strong — Celenneth, Hanar, Dagrun, Ida, Ginar, Nar, Regin, and the two new-freed, Lif and Tora — down into passages none of them had walked, and the deep closed over them again as it had before. This was blind country even to the dwarves: no named halls now, no map behind the eyes, only the torch and the next turning and the patient ranger's work of leaving marks to find the way back by. They went slow and watchful, pushing into the dark's own ground, and every branching of the way was a choice made half on instinct.

The first day's road ended at a dead wall — but the wall was a gift. A shear in the rock high above had let the deep bleed water into the low places, drowning what had once been a sizable orc-nest, and the flood stood clear and cold in the ruin of it. They stood at its edge with their torches doubled in the black surface and wondered what fed it from higher up, and took the wondering as a sign to keep climbing.

On the second day the going eased, and by its middle they came on a remote outpost and more of Balin's own — Frerin, Fenja, Yrr, and Sefa, of an earlier expedition sent down and half-forgotten. They had taken in two dwarves the goblins had kept as thralls, Svior and Veig, found wandering the dark half-mad and raving of mansions of gold hidden somewhere below. Celenneth gave the two what herb-craft she had, and it did little for minds the deep had broken. But there is strength in numbers in the dark, and the two parties threw in together and went on as one.

After that the search wore at them. Svior and Veig had to be tended and half-carried, and failed a little more with every league deeper; the passages seemed to circle back on themselves, though Celenneth's marks swore they never crossed their own trail; a tunnel had fallen in on itself and cost them the better part of a day's doubling-back, and the dwarves' patience wore to a thread. But on the fifth day, up a hard climb at the very end of their strength, they found it — a pool of clean water fed by a thin thread of a waterfall spilling down out of the dark above, untouched by any orc: a small clean mercy, a living thing, deep in the dead heart of the mountain. They stood around it a moment and none of them spoke, and then they drank, and it was sweet.[^r-search]

They made their camp around the pool, settled Svior and Veig to what rest their minds allowed, set watches on the dark, and sent a party back to Balin with word and a call for supplies and more hands. In the days that followed they built the place out into something defensible, and there was a plain, worn satisfaction in the labour of it — a clean well won back from the deep, a second hearth kindled where the orcs had held nothing but poison.

Rest came harder to Celenneth than the work did. Each night her sleep turned to dark dreams — endless tunnels, a voice in the shadows shaping her name, unseen eyes at the edge of the light — and she woke uneasy each time, the weight of the place still on her.[^s-dreams] Dagrun was changed by it too; the long campaign and the deeper dark had cooled something in her, and she smoked her pipe in a long silence now, her gaze gone far off to somewhere none of them could follow.

Still, they had done it: a haven made in the heart of Moria, a foothold reclaimed for Balin's company. More lay ahead, Celenneth knew. But this was worth the marking.

*Lótessë 17, 2965*

The weeks turned the camp into a settlement. Parties came and went along the road they had opened between the First Hall and the new outpost, bearing gear and stores and news, and the deep between the two grew almost familiar with the walking of it. The First Hall was still the wider and the easier held — but it had no water, and this place did, the lifeblood of any place folk meant to hold. A hard-won thing, and it gave them all something to hope on.

[^r-search]: **Journey** into the lower deeps (nine, then merged with Frerin's party) to find clean water — collapsed tunnels, circling passages, days of hard going. The rescued thralls **Svior and Veig** raved of "mansions of gold" hidden below — a thread that will lead to the fortress of Malech.
[^s-dreams]: The deep's **Shadow** works on Celenneth in sleep — recurring dark dreams, a voice shaping her name.
