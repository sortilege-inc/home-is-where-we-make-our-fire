# Chapter 8: New Frontiers / Base Camp

*Book 1*

*Víressë 28, 2965*

The orcs had been pushed back, their attack repelled, and Celenneth had taken time to rest and recover from her wounds. The days were filled with tending to the camp, strengthening defenses, and finding moments of solace amidst the grim surroundings. It was during one of these quiet moments that Balin approached her, Hanar walking beside him.

"Celenneth," Balin began, his tone direct. "We need to establish a secondary source of water, deeper into Moria—something untainted by the filth of the orcs. Somewhere we can make a more permanent base camp." He glanced at Hanar before continuing. "I’m tasking you two, along with your crew, to find it. Lifa and Tora, two of the dwarves you rescued, want to join you as well. Expect resistance; you'll likely have to cut through enemy lines."

Celenneth nodded, the weight of the responsibility settling over her. She exchanged a glance with Hanar, who gave a resolute nod. "We’ll get it done," she said simply.

Preparations were quickly made. Supplies were gathered, weapons sharpened, and the group assembled. They set out with a war party of nine—Celenneth, Hanar, Dagrin, Ida, Ginar, Nar, Regan, and the two newly joined Lifa and Tora. Each of them was ready for the challenge ahead, their eyes hardened by the trials they had already faced.

The first day took them ten miles into the depths of Moria, the passageways twisting and turning, the darkness thick and unyielding. They hit a dead end late in the day, but what seemed like a setback turned into an opportunity. A shear in the rock above had caused a flood, drowning what appeared to have once been a sizable orc nest below. The water pooled around them, clear and cold, and they wondered if there was a source higher up that they could reach.

They made camp that night, fatigue setting in as they rested among the flooded ruins. The journey was already proving arduous, but the hope of finding a clean source of water drove them forward.

On the second day, they made better progress, traveling another eight miles without incident. The path grew easier, the air less oppressive, and by midday, they stumbled upon a remote outpost—a place where they met other dwarves.

Frerin, Fenja, Yrr, and Sefa were part of an earlier expedition from Balin. They had been traveling through Moria when they found Svior and Veig—two dwarves who had been taken as thralls by the goblins. The two were in a pitiable state, half-mad and rambling about mansions of gold hidden within the ruins. Frerin and the others had managed to subdue them, hoping to find a way to help.

Celenneth offered what little healing she could, using herbs she had gathered, but the effects were minimal. Still, the presence of the additional dwarves was a comfort, and they decided to merge their groups, strength in numbers being their greatest advantage.

The third day saw their progress slow. Svior and Veig needed constant care, their strength waning as they moved deeper into Moria. Fatigue began to wear on the group, and though they covered six more miles, they seemed to be traveling in circles. Celenneth, however, had been marking key locations, and she was certain they had not been doubling back. They pressed on, determination etched in their faces.

On the fourth day, frustration began to set in. They covered another eight miles, only to hit a tunnel that had collapsed in on itself, blocking their way forward. Half a day was spent doubling back, searching for a new route. The dwarves muttered in irritation, their patience wearing thin, but they pushed on, knowing the mission was too important to abandon.

By the fifth day, exhaustion weighed heavily on them. The last few miles seemed endless, the path climbing sharply, their muscles straining with every step. But as the day drew to a close, they found what they were looking for—a pool of clean, clear water, fed by a small waterfall that trickled down from above. It seemed untouched by the orcs, a hidden gem deep within the mountain.

They set up camp around the pool, helping Svior and Veig settle in to rest and receive care. The dwarves took turns guarding the site, their weapons ready as they watched the darkness for any sign of movement. A small party was sent back to Balin's camp to bring more supplies and additional dwarves to establish the base.

The next few days were spent building out the camp, the dwarves working tirelessly to create a secure and defensible outpost. They reclaimed hope in the form of fresh water, and though the work was exhausting, there was a sense of accomplishment in what they had achieved.

But rest was fleeting. Each night, as Celenneth lay down to sleep, dark dreams plagued her—visions of endless tunnels, shadows that whispered her name, and unseen eyes watching from the darkness. She woke each morning with a sense of unease, the weight of the shadow still pressing down on her.

Dagrun, too, seemed changed by the journey. The constant battles and the strain of Moria had hardened her, her eyes losing some of their previous warmth. She smoked her pipe in silence, her gaze distant, as if lost in thought.

Despite the challenges, they had succeeded. The new base camp was established, a haven within the depths of Moria. They had reclaimed a safe place for Balin's company, a step forward in their quest to reclaim the ancient dwarven kingdom. Celenneth knew that there were more challenges ahead, but for now, they had achieved something worth celebrating.

*Lótessë 17, 2965*

Time passed, and the camp grew. Multiple parties moved between the First Hall and the new outpost, bringing equipment, supplies, and other goods. The First Hall remained more spacious, easier to defend, but this new location had something the First Hall lacked—fresh water, the lifeblood of any settlement. It was a hard-won victory, but one that gave them hope for the future.
