# Chapter 7: The Ledge of Woe

*Víressë 4, 2965*

On the sixth day they found the passage to the Ledge of Woe, and the crying of the captives came up it louder, off the wet stone. Orcs went in and out of the place with a careless frequency that told its own story — but the filth of the flooded halls still clung to the company, and it masked them, and they came up close unseen.

The ledge itself was a cruelty. The captives — dwarves, and even a few orcs — had been herded onto a shelf above the drop, and their captors were making sport of it, shoving and jeering, setting them to fight one another for the right to keep their footing. Celenneth counted the guard from the shadows and did not like the sum.[^r-count] The great brute among them she would come to know as Grimnar the Gaoler.

The first thing was the guard post, and the silencing of it before any orc could run for help. Balin gave the smallest nod, and Celenneth went.

She led them in before the last of the prisoners had been driven through the door, and in the cramped stone of the guardhouse it became at once a butcher's press of bodies. She came up against the orc chieftain and drew first blood — and paid for it, his blade opening her shoulder near the breast, so the room swam and she gave ground fighting to keep her feet.[^r-assault] Hanar threw himself in with his axe going in wide arcs, but the crush split them apart, dwarf from dwarf, each walled into his own small battle. She could not make her voice carry over it. Hanar cut his way back to the others and hewed a path for them to close ranks again.

Cut off, she did the thing she did best: nocked, and loosed, and put the arrow into Grimnar's shoulder and staggered the brute. It bought the dwarves their footing. They pressed, and on the ledge the captives took heart at the sight of their own kin and turned on the guards, and three orcs went screaming off the shelf into the dark. Celenneth drew again, and this time the shaft took Grimnar through the eye, and the Gaoler went down like a felled post. The rest broke — and she called out over the din, *let none of them run* — and the dwarves saw to it that none did.[^r-grimnar]

They brought the six captives back through the gate, left two bound and silent orcs behind, and stripped the guardhouse quick: a little treasure, and in Grimnar's quarters a dwarven dagger, wickedly keen. Then they went, laden with what food and dwarf-wine they could trust in that place, the load shared out so the weakened could keep the pace.

The way back was its own trial, for they went slower now — burdened with six freed captives weak from their keeping, and a company that had already spent itself in the taking of them. Celenneth cut the road short where the deep allowed it: her eye found a plunging watercourse, and they rode it down on a scavenged orc-craft, a whole day's march gone in a single racing hour of white water and dark; and later a network of great hollow pipes whose throats carried the sound of the warbands to them long before the warbands came, so they could go softly another way.[^r-return] But the deep does not give up its distances cleanly. The stairs ran on past counting, the ledges narrow and dizzying under weary feet, the rescued dwarves flagging until they had to be let to rest whether the company willed it or no. A dead end sent them doubling back over ground they had no strength to spare. The last four miles cost them near a whole day. But at the end of it the great pillars of the First Hall rose up out of the dark like the gateposts of home, and Balin's camp met them with a cheer that went a long way toward mending what the road had taken.

She had earned her place, and knew it. They gave her food and healing herbs and leave to mend, and Hanar was given the keen dagger for his courage in the guardhouse.

The rest did not last. An orc assault came howling against the camp's defences not long after, and Celenneth rose to it weary and met it anyway — and came out the other side harder than she had gone in, something in her tempered by all of it, her eye keener in the dark, her will set deeper than before.[^r-camp] The shadow of Moria still lay over the place. But she would not break under it, and neither would they.

[^r-count]: The Ledge of Woe garrison — an orc chieftain, a great orc bodyguard (**Grimnar the Gaoler**), four orc guards, three goblin archers, and four orc soldiers, one bearing the mark of **Udûn**, a cruel secret order of Moria-orcs. A dozen and more.
