# Chapter 5: Bilbo & the Ring

*Cermië 9, 2969*

The warmth of Bag End drifted over Linnea like a welcome as she stepped inside — the hall lit by a soft copper-shaded lamp, the scent of pipe-weed and dried herbs on the air, a hearth crackling somewhere ahead. After weeks on the road she let it settle into her bones.

Bilbo came round a corner, his face lighting at the sight of Gandalf. "There you are! I thought I heard your voice."

"It's been too long, Bilbo," Gandalf said.

Bilbo's gaze slid to Linnea with frank curiosity. "And who might this be?"

"Linnea," she said, meeting his eyes. "Thank you for letting me in."

"Any friend of Gandalf's is welcome in Bag End." He led them to a snug sitting room crammed with books, poured tea into small cups with the care of a treasured ritual, and settled back. "Now — I know Gandalf, of course. But nothing of you. Where are you from, and what brings you to the Shire?"

Linnea sipped the warm, floral tea. "I'm from Bree. My father was a merchant there. I never meant to leave home for long, but life leads you places you never expected."

"Bree, eh? Friendly folk. Not many wander far. How did you end up all the way west?"

"I was heading east, first," she said, wry. "This is the second half of a journey that looped through more places than I ever meant to see. I've been as far as Dale, and through Mirkwood, and I spent time in Rivendell." Bilbo's eyebrows climbed, delight creeping in — he had been to both Dale and Rivendell, he said, and there was nowhere like the latter — and when he asked whether she had ever gone as far south as Rohan, her expression softened. "I have. A proud land. They treated me well, once they knew I meant no harm."

"You've wandered a fair piece of the map." He tilted his head. "Did you travel alone all that while?"

Her gaze drifted to the fire. "Not always. I met a Ranger — Celenneth. She saved my life when bandits took me on the road south of Bree. Without her, I might not be here." And when he asked if she had stayed with the Ranger, her smile widened. "We did more than stay together. We built a family. We have a daughter, Brynja, safe and waiting for me. Everything I do, I do with her in mind."

"A daughter! How wonderful." Bilbo's eyes were bright. "That gives you reason to keep traveling — and reason to know when to head home."

"It does," she said softly. "She gives me hope."

For a moment the three of them lapsed into a comfortable quiet, the hearth crackling. Then Bilbo cleared his throat. "Well — I don't mean to pry. But you must have a reason for coming to Hobbiton. You didn't stumble in by chance."

Before Linnea could answer, Gandalf set down his tea and gave the Hobbit a meaningful look. "We have matters to discuss, old friend. Matters that need your particular perspective."

Bilbo straightened, glancing between them. "My perspective? I can't imagine what I'd know that a wizard and a world-traveler would need."

Gandalf only smiled and touched the pipe in his vest. "Let's say you've had certain experiences that might shed light on a question or two."

Linnea decided to trust him. She drew off her glove and held out her hand, and the simple silver ring on her finger caught the firelight, its faint runes hinting at old craft. "This is why," she said. "I've carried it some time. It came to me from a spirit, I think — a wandering presence near the Anduin. It never felt dangerous. Just… sad. I'd like to know what it is."

The instant Bilbo saw it, his posture eased, relief washing over him. "Ah. Not mine," he said, and smiled almost apologetically. "For a moment I thought — well, never mind what I thought." He fetched a small lens and held the ring gently between thumb and forefinger, studying it in silence. "Hmm. Old. Possibly Second Age. Elven craft, but not the common kind. These runes… they're about holding on. A memory ring, maybe — made by someone who refused to let something vanish with time. Not a memory like yours or mine, but a piece of someone's essence. A promise, perhaps. A farewell. But I sense no darkness in it. Only a longing that never settled."

Gandalf let out a gentle stream of smoke. "And no resonance?" he asked, measured.

Bilbo shook his head. "No. It doesn't answer to… others I know of." He met Gandalf's gaze with a wry smile, and tapped his own waistcoat lightly, leaving the rest unsaid. "Whatever else it is, it's its own thing. Not connected."

Linnea caught the undercurrent but didn't pry; Gandalf had asked after resonance, and Bilbo seemed relieved her ring was not bound to whatever he kept close. That was enough.[^r-ring]

"So what do I do with it?" she asked.

Bilbo returned it. "It chose you. I doubt hiding it will help you understand it. Keep it close, listen to your instincts. Maybe one day it will make sense."

"Sometimes these old relics guide rather than harm," Gandalf added. "You said it came from a spirit. That spirit may have needed you to carry something forward — some memory they couldn't let die."

Linnea ran a thumb over the runes. "I've heard no voices, seen no visions. Only a feeling, now and then — like being watched, but never threatened." She slipped the ring back on, deciding to trust herself.

"Best leave it there," Gandalf said. "We've mystery enough to fill a dozen nights. No need to unravel it all this evening."

Bilbo set the lens aside and rose, leading them down a lantern-lit hall to a snug guest room with a small bed under a round window, moonlight silvering the floor. "Bag End has a room made for weary travelers. Get some sleep, my dear. Morning's a safer time for questions."

When he had gone, Linnea drew the wolf's-head clasp from her pouch and turned it over in the moonlight, the engraved head somehow significant — a story lurking just outside Bag End's warm walls. But Gandalf would have thoughts, and that could wait for sunrise. She tucked it away, pulled off her boots, and slid under a quilt heavier and softer than any forest floor. Outside, the Shire slumbered; no rushing wind, no creak of distant trees, no threat at the edge of firelight. For once she did not fight sleep, and it came gently, and no troubled dream reached for her. Within the quiet comfort of Bag End, with Gandalf and Bilbo near, Linnea slept deep and easy, and let the world's troubles spin silently beyond the round green door.

[^r-ring]: **Lore** (Bilbo) — Bilbo examines Linnea's spirit-given ring: old Elven craft, a "memory ring" made to hold a piece of someone's essence, with no darkness in it — and, to Gandalf's quiet relief, no *resonance* with a certain other ring the Hobbit keeps close. It is its own thing; Linnea keeps wearing it.
