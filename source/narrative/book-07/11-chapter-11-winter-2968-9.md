# Chapter 11: Winter 2968/9

*Nénimë 6, 2969*

The winter wind rattled the shutters of their small home in Edoras, carrying the sharp scent of snow where spring should have been. Brynja, just turned three winters old, dozed in the corner by the little hearth, wrapped in blankets. Outside, the low sun threw long shadows over the frost-laden thatch, and the murmur of stable hands drifted through the chilled air. Inside, all warmth seemed to have fled.

Linnea sat at the rough table, her palms pressed flat to it, her knuckles taut. Celenneth leaned against the far wall, arms crossed, her face shadowed. Between them stretched a silence thick with things unsaid.

"I've been thinking," Linnea began at last, steady but subdued, her eyes on Celenneth. "About Brynja. About what she needs."

Celenneth's posture stiffened. "And what do you think that is?"

Linnea's gaze dropped to the worn grain of the table, and her words came slow and deliberate. "Stability. A home where the ground doesn't shift every time you ride away. She needs a community, Celenneth — neighbors, friends, familiar faces over the years, not a string of passing strangers." She paused, softening. "I've been thinking of Bree. It isn't perfect. But it might be the kind of place where she could grow up rooted. What do you think?"

Celenneth straightened, uncrossing her arms. "Bree?" She sounded almost baffled. "You always hated how cramped it felt. The noise, the crowds. Why now?"

Linnea closed her eyes a moment, pressing her fingers into the wood. "It's not that I've forgotten those things. But Brynja's growing, learning her words, making sense of the world. She deserves a place where she can run the same courtyard day after day, where people know her name." She looked up. "And maybe we need that too. Somewhere to learn who we are when we're not always on the move."

Celenneth stepped nearer, resting her hands on the back of a chair as though it might anchor her. "Are you asking me to settle in Bree?" The edge in her had softened to something closer to confusion.

Linnea shook her head, a faint, bittersweet smile touching her lips. "I'm not saying Bree is the only answer. I'm only wondering if it's time we thought about something steadier. A place where Brynja can feel safe — where we can. But more than anything, Celenneth, I want to know what you want. For yourself. For us." Her voice gentled further. "I need to know you're here because you choose to be. Not because you feel bound to."

At that, Celenneth's grip tightened, her knuckles paling against the old chair. She opened her mouth, and closed it. Outside a stable door creaked, and a distant rider's call drifted in. Within, there was only the small hearth and Brynja's soft breathing.

"Linnea, I—" Celenneth's voice was barely above a whisper. "I don't know what to say."

Linnea rose and pushed her chair back without a sound, and crossed to the shuttered window, pressing her palm to the cold wood. In the quiet she could almost see the roads winding away from Edoras — east toward Bree, or south along Celenneth's old paths. "Then don't say anything," she murmured, not looking back. "Just think on it."

The silence returned, heavy and long, and Celenneth watched her, feeling the whole weight of the choice. The wind sighed through the thatch, and Brynja stirred but did not wake.
