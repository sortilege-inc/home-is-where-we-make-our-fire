# Chapter 1: Tending to One Another

*Cermië 11, 2966*

The house was quiet at last, the fire throwing its warm light across the walls. Freowyn had carried a worn-out Brynja to bed after an evening of tears and laughter, and left Celenneth and Linnea alone, the months apart sitting heavy in the silence between them.

Celenneth set aside her cloak and gear first, slow and deliberate, and when she unfastened the bindings of her tunic the raw marks on her wrists and the fading bruises on her arms came to light. Linnea's breath caught.

"Celenneth. What did they do to you?"

"Later," Celenneth murmured, not meeting her eyes, bending to her boots — and Linnea saw how stiffly the leg bent, favoring the crude splint.

"Your leg—"

"Broken. Fangorn wasn't kind."

Linnea's mouth thinned. She fetched a basin and a cloth. "Sit down." It brooked no argument, and Celenneth obeyed, sinking to the edge of the bed with a wince. Linnea knelt and unwound the splint, her hands steady though her heart was not, and began to wash the dirt and dried blood from her skin. Each bruise, each scar told its own hard story, and somewhere in the telling her composure cracked, and tears spilled over.

"It was so hard without you," she got out. "Every day I wondered if you were alive. Every day I had to decide whether to give Brynja hope her mother was coming home — and risk breaking her heart — or not."

"Linnea—"

"I tried to keep going. To be bright for her, to hold it all together. But inside I was coming apart. I thought I'd lost you for good."

Celenneth reached up and brushed her cheek. "I'm so sorry. I never meant to be gone so long."

"Then tell me what happened."

She let out a long breath. "I thought I could take a small detour on the way home — learn what I could along the road. But the Entwash flooded and cut me off. I looked for another way and walked into an orc patrol. They took me. Marched me toward Dol Guldur." She hesitated. "I escaped into Fangorn, and broke the leg there, in a fall."

Linnea's hands trembled at the marks on her wrists. "But the forest sheltered you?"

"It wasn't shelter. It was a test. Fangorn doesn't trust easily. I spent weeks only surviving — snares, foraging, mending. It let me go in the end. But not before I'd proved I wasn't what the Shadow had tried to make me."

"And the patrol?"

"They found me at the forest's edge. I didn't feel it was my place to demand they drop everything and carry me to Edoras. They had their own duties. I was trying not to be a burden."

Linnea nodded slowly, the tears softening to something gentler, and rested her brow against Celenneth's. "I thought you'd forgotten us. Forgotten me."

Celenneth cupped her face in both hands. "Never. You and Brynja were the only thing that kept me alive."

Linnea's voice dropped to a whisper. "When I saw you with Garin, I thought—" She couldn't finish it.

Celenneth's expression softened to understanding. "I was oblivious. I didn't see what she felt. And I'll be honest with you, because I always will be — had she asked me to share her bed, I don't know that I'd have thought to say no. And none of it would have felt like a betrayal to me. You hold my heart. You always will. That doesn't move."

Linnea searched her eyes, tears standing again. "I know you have a more... plain relationship with your body than I do. I even love that in you, though I don't know if I'll ever feel the same way about mine." Her voice trembled. "I just need to know I'll always matter to you."

Celenneth's grip gentled on her face. "You do. You always will. I swore it to you once. I'll swear it again as many times as you need."

Linnea's lips quirked, trembling. "You don't have to. Just stay. With me. With us."

Celenneth leaned in and pressed a kiss to her brow. "Of course, my love."

Linnea turned so her back settled against Celenneth's chest, and Celenneth wrapped her in her arms, and their breathing slowed into a single rhythm, and they slept still folded together.
