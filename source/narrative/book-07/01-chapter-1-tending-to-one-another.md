# Chapter 1: Tending to one another

*Book 7*

*Cermië 11, 2966*

The house was quiet now, the fire’s warm glow flickering across the walls. Freowyn had carried Brynja to bed after a long evening of tearful reunions and laughter, leaving Linnea and Celenneth alone. The silence between them was thick with unspoken words, the weight of months apart pressing down like a heavy shroud.

Celenneth removed her cloak and gear first, her movements slow and deliberate. Her fingers trembled as she unfastened the bindings on her tunic, revealing the raw marks on her wrists and the faint bruises on her arms. Linnea’s breath caught as she noticed them, her eyes widening with horror.

“Celenneth,” she whispered, stepping closer. “What did they do to you?”

“Later,” Celenneth murmured, avoiding Linnea’s gaze. Her focus remained on removing her boots, and Linnea’s attention shifted to the way her partner’s leg bent stiffly, favoring the crude splint.

“Your leg…” Linnea began, her voice trailing off.

Celenneth gave a faint, bitter smile. “Broken. Fangorn didn’t make things easy.”

Linnea’s lips pressed into a thin line as she reached for a basin of water and a cloth. “Sit down,” she said firmly, her tone brooking no argument.

Celenneth obeyed, sinking onto the edge of the bed with a quiet wince. Linnea knelt before her, her hands steady but her heart trembling. She gently unwrapped the splint, her fingers brushing over the rough edges of the makeshift bindings. Each bruise, each scar told its own harrowing story.

As Linnea worked, washing the dirt and blood from Celenneth’s skin, her own composure cracked. Tears welled in her eyes, spilling over as she tried to speak. “It was so hard without you,” she choked out. “Every day, I wondered if you were alive. Every day I would have to decide whether to give Brynja hope that Mama was coming back soon, and risk her disappointment, or break her heart.”

Celenneth’s hands stilled over her lap. “Linnea,” she began softly, but Linnea shook her head, her tears falling freely now.

“I tried to keep going,” Linnea continued, her voice trembling. “I tried to be cheerful for Brynja, to hold everything together. But inside, I was breaking. I thought I’d lost you forever.”

Celenneth reached out, her hand brushing against Linnea’s cheek. “I’m so sorry,” she whispered. “I never wanted to be gone this long.”

“Then tell me what happened,” Linnea said, her voice both pleading and commanding.

Celenneth exhaled deeply, her gaze distant as she began. “I thought I could take a small detour home, maybe find useful intel along the way. But the Entwash flooded, cutting off my route. I tried to find another way and ran straight into an orc patrol.” She hesitated, her voice growing softer. “They captured me. Took me to one of their camps. I eventually escaped into Fangorn Forest to the northwest, but I broke my leg in an accident there.”

Linnea’s hands trembled as she cleaned the marks on Celenneth’s wrists. “But the forest kept you safe?” she asked quietly.

“It wasn’t a refuge,” Celenneth admitted. “It was a test. The forest doesn’t trust easily. I spent weeks just surviving, setting snares, foraging, recovering strength. It let me leave eventually, but not before I… proved myself.”

Linnea’s fingers stilled. “And the patrol?”

“They found me on the edge of Fangorn,” Celenneth said. “I didn’t feel it was my place to demand they take me to Edoras immediately. They had their own responsibilities, and I was trying to not be a burden.”

Linnea nodded slowly, the tears in her eyes softening to something gentler—a mix of understanding and lingering sorrow. She rested her forehead against Celenneth’s. “I thought you’d forgotten us. Forgotten me.”

Celenneth’s brows knitted, and she cupped Linnea’s face in her hands. “Never,” she said firmly. “You and Brynja were the only thing keeping me alive.”

Linnea hesitated, her voice barely a whisper. “When I saw you with Garin, I thought…” She trailed off, unable to finish the sentence.

Celenneth’s expression shifted to one of soft understanding. “I was oblivious,” she admitted. “I didn’t see what she felt. But had she asked to be physical, I don't know that I would've thought to say no. Nothing about that would have felt like a betrayal to you, in my mind. You will always hold my heart.”

Linnea’s eyes searched hers, tears welling again. “I know you have a more... functional relationship to your body; I appreciate that about you, even if I'm not sure if I'll ever feel the same way,” she said, her voice trembling. “I just want to know that I’ll always be important to you.”

Celenneth’s grip tightened gently on Linnea’s face. “You are. You always will be. I swore it to you once, and I’ll swear it again if I must.”

Linnea’s lips quirked in a faint, trembling smile. “You don’t have to,” she murmured. “Just stay with me. With us.”

Celenneth leaned forward, pressing a kiss to her forehead. “Of course, my love.”

Linnea turned so that her back faced Celenneth, and Celenneth wrapped her arms around her, their breaths and heartbeats steadying into a shared rhythm. As they drifted into sleep, their embrace remained.
