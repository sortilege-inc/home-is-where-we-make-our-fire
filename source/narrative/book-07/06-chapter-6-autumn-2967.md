# Chapter 6: Autumn 2967

*Book 7*

*Autumn 2967*

The kitchen was filled with the fragrant scent of dried herbs as Linnea carefully crushed chamomile flowers in a small, carved mortar. Freowyn stood across the table, her arms crossed and an eyebrow quirked in curiosity. The warm afternoon light streamed through the window, casting golden streaks over the bundles of plants hanging from the ceiling.

“So this one,” Linnea said, her voice calm and steady, “is for soothing pain and helping with sleep. Chamomile works well in teas or poultices, but you have to be careful not to mix it with anything too strong. It’s gentle but effective.”

Freowyn leaned in, nodding slowly as she studied the crushed petals. “And this is common in Bree, is it?” she asked, her tone thoughtful.

Linnea smiled faintly. “Very. My grandmother used to grow it just outside our home. She always said there wasn’t a problem that couldn’t be eased with a good cup of chamomile tea.”

Freowyn reached for a sprig of thyme on the table, twirling it between her fingers. “And what about this one? I’ve used it for cooking, but you’ve mentioned it has other uses.”

“It’s wonderful for disinfecting wounds,” Linnea replied. “You can make a wash or a salve with it. It’s especially helpful for minor cuts and scrapes—and for calming an upset stomach when brewed as a tea.”

Freowyn’s eyes lit up with interest. “I’ve seen enough scrapes and bruises in the stables to make good use of that.”

Their lessons became a regular part of their routine. Linnea would guide Freowyn through the preparation of tinctures, salves, and teas, sharing knowledge she had gleaned from her time in Bree and her travels with Celenneth. In return, Freowyn began teaching Linnea skills she had honed over years in Rohan.

It began with the horses.

“A foal is no different from a soldier,” Freowyn explained one day as they stood in the stables. The mare before them was heavily pregnant, her breathing slow and labored. “Both need guidance and care from the moment they enter this world. It’s not just about what you do with your hands, but about being steady and calm. They feel everything you feel.”

Linnea listened intently, her brow furrowed as Freowyn showed her how to prepare the birthing area with clean hay and water. When the foal came, Freowyn’s steady hands and soothing voice guided Linnea through the process—checking the mare, clearing the foal’s nose and mouth, and ensuring both mother and baby were safe and healthy.

Afterward, as they watched the foal take its first wobbly steps, Linnea let out a breath she hadn’t realized she’d been holding. “That was… incredible,” she murmured.

Freowyn grinned, wiping her hands on a cloth. “You did well. And once you’ve seen a few more births, it’ll feel as natural as mixing one of your tinctures.”

Over time, Freowyn’s lessons expanded to include more than just horses. She began to teach Linnea the basics of midwifery for humans, starting with herbal remedies to ease labor pains and prevent infections.

“This isn’t just about birth,” Freowyn said one evening as they worked together over a bundle of comfrey. “It’s about life. About being there in the moments when someone is most vulnerable and helping them find their strength.”

Linnea absorbed every word, her hands steady as she prepared a poultice under Freowyn’s guidance. The more she learned, the more she saw the parallels between their crafts—the quiet care of herbalism and the hands-on compassion of midwifery. Both required patience, intuition, and an unwavering commitment to those in need.
