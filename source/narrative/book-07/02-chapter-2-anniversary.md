# Chapter 2: Anniversary

*Book 7*

*Narvinyë 12, 2967*

The midwinter chill had begun to soften as the Yule festivities came to an end in Edoras, and with it came a quieter, more personal celebration in the household of Linnea and Celenneth. The anniversary of the day they had found Brynja, abandoned and vulnerable, was upon them—a day that had forever changed their lives. It was a time to honor not only their daughter but the unexpected journey that had made them a family.

Freowyn bustled about the home, her sleeves rolled up as she prepared a hearty stew, the aroma of rosemary and roasted roots filling the air. “We’ll need enough for a feast,” she declared, her voice bright with purpose. “Even if it’s just us, it should feel like a proper celebration.”

Linnea stood by the hearth, her hands deftly stringing small dried flowers onto a length of twine. “In Bree, we would hang garlands like this to mark a child’s first birthday,” she said, a faint smile touching her lips. “It’s said to bring blessings for the year ahead.”

“And in the North,” Celenneth added, her voice soft but warm, “we would leave a circle of bread and fruit on the doorstep—a gift to the spirits, to thank them for watching over the child.” She set about arranging a small offering near the door, her movements careful and reverent.

Brynja, wide-eyed and curious, darted between them, her small hands occasionally reaching for the garland or the bread. “For me?” she asked, her voice tinged with wonder.

“Every bit of it,” Linnea said, scooping her up and planting a kiss on her cheek. “Because today is the day we celebrate finding you, little star.”

As the sun dipped low and the golden light bathed the room, a few close friends of the household arrived. Eadwyn, a Rohirrim that Linnea had been training with, brought a carved wooden horse for Brynja, its polished surface gleaming in the firelight. Hild, a neighbor and friend of Freowyn’s, arrived with a basket of freshly baked honey cakes.

“A child of Rohan must have a horse of her own,” Eadwyn said with a grin as she handed the carving to Brynja. “Even if it’s a small one for now.”

Brynja hugged the gift tightly, her face lighting up. “Horsey!”

Freowyn laughed, setting the stew on the table. “That’s a fine name. But come now, it’s time to eat before the cakes disappear.”

The meal was a blending of their cultures. The hearty stew was paired with a flatbread recipe from Celenneth's ranger days, while Linnea had prepared a dessert inspired by Bree’s sweet berry puddings. The table was adorned with small tokens from their travels: a polished stone from the North, a delicate Rohan weave, and a wooden trinket carved in Bree.

After the meal, they gathered around the fire for stories. Freowyn began with a tale of a courageous mare who saved her rider in battle, while Celenneth followed with a ranger’s fable of stars guiding lost travelers home. Linnea voice was steady as she shared a Bree story about the strength of unlikely families, her eyes lingering on Brynja as she spoke.

When the stories ended, Linnea stood and took a small silver pendant from her pocket, its surface engraved with a simple star. “This is for you, Brynja,” she said, fastening it gently around the child’s neck. “A reminder that you are our light, even on the darkest nights.”

Brynja’s fingers touched the pendant, her expression serious for a moment before she broke into a wide smile. “Big girl now?”

“It means you’re our girl,” Celenneth said, pulling her into a warm embrace.

As the night deepened, the fire crackled softly, and the household fell into a peaceful rhythm. Brynja dozed in Linnea’s lap, her small fingers clutching her new pendant. Freowyn hummed a quiet tune as she tidied the kitchen, and Eadwyn leaned back in her chair, a rare softness in her eyes as she watched the family.

Celenneth reached for Linnea’s hand, her thumb brushing gently over her knuckles. “We’re lucky to have her,” she murmured.

Linnea nodded, her gaze resting on Brynja. “And lucky to have each other.”

For a moment, the weight of the world seemed far away, and all that remained was the warmth of their home and the love that bound them together. It was a day of celebration, not just for Brynja, but for the family they had built against all odds.
