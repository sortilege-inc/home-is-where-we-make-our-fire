# Chapter 5: Summer 2967

*Book 7*

*Summer 2967*

The morning air was crisp and filled with the faint scent of fresh hay and damp earth as Linnea adjusted the saddle on a chestnut mare. Her hands worked deftly, the movements practiced now after months of training. Eadwyn, tall and broad-shouldered with a braid that swung like a rope of gold, leaned casually against the fence, watching with a critical eye.

“You’ve improved,” Eadwyn said, her voice carrying a note of approval. “Your hands are steady, and the horse trusts you more.”

Linnea glanced over her shoulder, a faint smile tugging at her lips. “You say that like it’s a miracle.”

“For someone who once held the reins like they might bite her? It is.” Eadwyn grinned, pushing off the fence and stepping closer. “Now, let’s see how you ride today. I want to work on your speed and balance.”

Mounting the mare with ease, Linnea took a deep breath, her hands light on the reins. She had been training with Eadwyn since shortly after Celenneth had left for Gondor, but the sessions had become more frequent in recent months. With Celenneth mostly staying in Edoras now, Linnea felt more determined than ever to hone her skills—to be someone who could stand at her partner’s side in strength as well as spirit.

Eadwyn swung up onto her own horse, a sleek black gelding, and gave a sharp whistle. “Let’s ride!”

The two of them took off across the open field, the cool wind whipping past their faces. Linnea’s heart raced as the mare’s hooves pounded the earth, her body moving instinctively with the rhythm of the horse. Eadwyn called out instructions, her voice clear over the thundering hooves.

“Lean into the turn! Keep your heels down—good! Now faster!”

Linnea obeyed, her movements growing more fluid with each ride. She could feel the difference in herself—the strength in her legs, the steadiness in her hands, the growing confidence that replaced the fear she had once felt. When they slowed to a trot near the far fence, Eadwyn nodded approvingly.

“You’re riding like a proper Rohirrim now,” she said with a grin. “At this rate, you’ll be outpacing me by next spring.”

Linnea laughed, brushing stray hair from her face. “Let’s not get carried away. I still have a lot to learn.”

The morning’s training didn’t stop with riding. After cooling the horses and returning them to the stables, Linnea and Eadwyn moved to the training grounds. The clang of swords rang out as they practiced footwork and strikes, Eadwyn’s blade flashing in the sunlight as she demonstrated a new technique.

“Your footwork is better,” Eadwyn said as Linnea parried an incoming blow. “But you’re hesitating. Don’t second-guess yourself. Commit to the strike.”

Linnea nodded, her brow furrowed in concentration. She adjusted her stance, her weight shifting as she struck forward with precision. This time, Eadwyn stepped back, a smile breaking across her face.

“That’s it,” Eadwyn said. “No hesitation. You’re getting there.”

The final part of their training took place on the archery range. Linnea stood with her feet planted firmly, her fingers steady on the bowstring. Eadwyn watched closely as Linnea aimed at the target ahead.

“Breathe,” Eadwyn instructed. “In through your nose, out through your mouth. Let the bow become an extension of you.”

Linnea released the arrow, and it struck the target—slightly off-center, but closer than she had managed a month ago. She exhaled, a sense of satisfaction washing over her.

“Good,” Eadwyn said, clapping her on the shoulder. “Soon, you’ll be hitting the bullseye every time.”

As the sun climbed higher in the sky, the two women sat near the edge of the training grounds, drinking water and catching their breath. Linnea stretched her legs, their soreness a reminder of how unfamiliar this all was.

“You’ve come a long way,” Eadwyn said, her tone more serious now. “I don’t say that lightly. There’s strength in you, Linnea. The kind that’s not just about swords or bows or horses. It’s the kind that makes people want to follow you.”

Linnea looked at her, surprised. “I’m not trying to lead anyone. I just want to be strong enough to protect the people I love.”

Eadwyn nodded, a small smile playing at her lips. “That’s exactly why people would follow you. But for now, keep training. You’re becoming something incredible.”

Linnea’s smile grew, and she glanced toward the horizon where the fields stretched endlessly. She thought of Celenneth and Brynja, of the family they were building, and the strength she would need to help protect it.

“Thank you, Eadwyn,” she said, her voice quiet but firm. “For everything.”

Eadwyn shrugged, her grin returning. “Just wait until you’re sparring circles around me. Then you can thank me.”

As they laughed, the bond between them grew stronger, built on shared effort and mutual respect. For Linnea, these moments were not just about learning the skills of the Rohirrim but about discovering her own potential—and realizing that she was more than capable of standing tall in a world that demanded strength and courage.
