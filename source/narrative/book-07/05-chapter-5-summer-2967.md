# Chapter 5: Summer 2967

*Cermië 12, 2967*

The morning air was crisp with the smell of fresh hay and damp earth as Linnea cinched the saddle on a chestnut mare, her hands moving deftly now after months of it. Eadwyn leaned against the fence, her gold braid swinging, watching with a critical eye.

"You've improved," she said, and meant it. "Your hands are steady. The horse trusts you more."

Linnea glanced over her shoulder, half a smile on her. "You say that like it's a miracle."

"For someone who once held the reins as though they might bite? It is." Eadwyn pushed off the fence. "Now let's see you ride. Speed and balance today."

Linnea swung up and took a breath, her hands light. She had been training with Eadwyn since not long after Celenneth first left for Gondor, and the sessions had only grown more frequent — for with Celenneth mostly settled in Edoras now, Linnea was more set than ever on honing herself, on being someone who could stand at her partner's side in strength as well as spirit.

Eadwyn vaulted onto her own black gelding and gave a sharp whistle. "Ride!" They tore off across the open field, the wind snatching at them, and Linnea's heart raced as the mare's hooves pounded the earth and her body found the rhythm of it. "Lean into the turn! Heels down — good! Now faster!" She obeyed, and grew more fluid with every pass, feeling the change in herself — strength in her legs, steadiness in her hands, a confidence where the old fear had been. When they slowed to a trot at the far fence, Eadwyn nodded. "You ride like a proper Rohir now. Keep this up and you'll outpace me by next spring."

Linnea laughed. "Let's not get carried away. I've a long way yet."

The morning did not end at the saddle. They cooled the horses and moved to the training ground, where swords rang as they worked footwork and strikes. "Your footwork's better," Eadwyn said as Linnea turned a blow aside. "But you hesitate. Don't second-guess. Commit to the strike." Linnea set her stance and struck true, and Eadwyn stepped back, grinning. "That's it. No hesitation." And after, at the archery butts, Eadwyn watched her nock and draw. "Breathe. In through the nose, out through the mouth. Let the bow be part of you." Linnea loosed, and the arrow struck the target — off-center, but nearer than she'd have managed a month gone, and she let out a satisfied breath.[^r-train]

As the sun climbed they sat at the edge of the ground, catching their breath, Linnea's legs aching pleasantly with the strangeness of it all. "You've come a long way," Eadwyn said, more serious now. "I don't say that lightly. There's a strength in you, Linnea — not only of swords and bows and horses. The kind that makes folk want to follow."

Linnea looked at her, surprised. "I'm not trying to lead anyone. I only want to be strong enough to protect the people I love."

Eadwyn's mouth curved. "That's exactly why they'd follow you. But for now — keep training. You're becoming something rare."

Linnea's smile grew, and she looked out to where the fields ran on without end, and thought of Celenneth and Brynja, and the family they were building, and the strength she would need to help keep it whole. "Thank you, Eadwyn. For everything."

Eadwyn shrugged, the grin returning. "Thank me when you're sparring circles around me."

[^r-train]: **Advancement (Rohan)** — through the year Linnea trains hard under Eadwyn in the three crafts of the Mark: riding, blade, and bow. Her competence and confidence climb well past where they began.
