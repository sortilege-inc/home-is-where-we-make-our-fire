# The Chronicle of Celenneth — Story Bible

*The single source of truth for the corpus. Every chapter is measured against this
document, and — ultimately — rewritten to it, book by book, chapter by chapter.*

---

## 0. North Star

**What this is.** A **romantasy epic** set in the Third Age of Middle-earth, grown from a
solo *The One Ring 2e* campaign. It is a full epic — journey, war, politics, grief, and a
central love story — that **veers, when the story earns it, into erotica**. It is *not*
merely erotica, and it is *not* Tolkien pastiche.

**Prose model.** We write in the register of **Samantha Shannon** (*The Priory of the Orange
Tree*, *A Day of Fallen Night*), not archaic Tolkien imitation: immersive close third-person,
rotating between a small set of anchored viewpoints; lush but *precise* sensory prose; deep,
disciplined interiority; political and mythic scope carried in a modern, elevated syntax;
romance and desire rendered frankly and with the same craft as everything else.

**The goal.** One cohesive work. The corpus is uneven because it was written across a long
campaign; the mandate is to make every chapter feel authored by one hand. We will **rewrite
the entire corpus chapter by chapter** to the standards below — not merely copyedit it.

---

## 1. Point of View

- **Third-person limited. One viewpoint per scene.** The viewpoint is fixed for the length of
  a scene and only ever changes at a **scene break** (§8).
- **Default viewpoint: Celenneth.** But this is a **rotating-POV epic** in the Shannon mode:
  where the story genuinely leaves her, a chapter or scene may be anchored to **Linnea**
  (all of Book 9 is hers), and occasionally **Brynja** or another established character.
- The anchor is chosen deliberately and held. **No mid-scene head-hopping. No omniscient
  intrusions** ("unknown to them…", authorial asides, stat-sheet blurbs).
- Interiority belongs to the viewpoint character only. We may *infer* others' feelings from
  what the viewpoint character observes, never *narrate* them from inside.

## 2. Tense

- **Past tense** for all narrative.
- **Present tense** is reserved for a distinct, signposted mode — **visions and prophetic
  dreams** — set in *italics* and applied consistently. Ordinary dreams and memories stay in
  past tense (past-perfect where needed for flashback).
- Direct interior thought may be *italicized*; keep its tense consistent chapter to chapter.

## 3. Register & Tone

- **Immersive and sensory, but economical.** Every image should earn its place. Elevated,
  contemporary syntax — not "thee/thou" archaism, not breathless purple.
- **Show, don't tell.** Earn emotion through concrete, specific detail; do not label it
  ("her heart swelled", "fury and hurt warred inside her").
- **Guardrails against the corpus's known tics:**
  - Cap the **"her X, her Y, her Z" absolute-phrase cadence** — the chained participial
    clauses ("her eyes widening, her lips parting, her fingers brushing…"). Use sparingly.
  - Retire the **stock similes** ("like a bow drawn taut", "like molten fire", "like a
    stormcloud", "world forgotten").
  - **Vary sentence openers.** No weather-tableau chapter openings by default; no monotone
    run of name-first / "She…" sentences; no "On the Nth day…" travelogue rhythm.
  - Prefer **scene over summary** for events — but see the Journey principle below: travel is
    an event, not the "rest." Compress the flat *mileage-log* ("on the third day they went eight
    miles"), never the journey itself.
- **Diction is in-world.** No modern/anachronistic slang ("kids", "intel", "sleeping bag").
- **The Journey matters (Tolkien / One Ring).** Travel is not connective tissue to be hurried
  through — in this tradition the road *is* the story: the land, the weather, the passage of
  days, the endurance and small mercies of the crossing, the way the wild is a Ranger's true
  element. Give journeys their page-space and their texture — landscape rendered as lived
  experience, the toll and the wonder of it — the way Tolkien lingers on the walk to Rivendell
  or the dark of Moria. What we cut is only the *mechanical mileage-log* (the "day one… day
  two… X miles" list), not the journey. When in doubt, a journey earns *more* room, not less.
- **Calibration: economical, not compressed.** Favour the short, load-bearing sentence over
  the cumulative pile-up — but **do not cut to a word target.** Trim fat; leave (or *expand*)
  what earns its length. Some scenes need only a light touch; some want room. **Consistent
  pacing across chapters is the priority**, not uniform leanness. Reference exemplar for the
  prose voice: rewritten **Book 1, Ch 1** (`editorial/pilot-b1c1.md`).

## 4. Intimacy & Adult Content

- The romance and its physical life are **core to the work**, not an appendage. Intimate and
  explicit scenes get the **same literary care** as a battle or a council: grounded in
  character, consensual and shown as such, sensual and specific — never clinical, never
  purple, never gratuitous filler.
- **Graphic level — aim high (~8.5/10).** When a scene is explicit, *render* it: specific
  physical and anatomical detail, the acts shown rather than faded or handed to euphemism.
  Don't pull the camera away at the moment. The craft is in keeping that explicitness
  literary and character-driven, not in softening it. Reference exemplar for the graphic
  register: rewritten **Book 11, Ch 5** (`editorial/pilot-b11c5.md`).
- **Explicitness is preserved at its intended level** (tender chapters stay tender; graphic
  chapters go fully graphic). Age-appropriate *gating* is handled by Jordan outside this
  repository; it is not a prose concern. Reference exemplar for the tender register: rewritten
  **Book 2, Ch 11** (`editorial/pilot-b2c11.md`).
- Recurring erotic scenes must **not** reuse the same choreography and verbatim refrains from
  chapter to chapter — vary them as you would any other repeated situation.

## 5. Mechanics as Footnotes — the actual-play apparatus

This work has an actual-play spine, and we honour it **without letting mechanics touch the
prose.** Game-mechanical events are lifted **out of the narrative and into footnotes.**

- **The prose never names a mechanic.** No "Weary", "Endurance", "Hope point", "a successful
  test", "she rolled", "a short rest", "advantage", or reward/virtue/item labels ("Nurhael's
  Warden Blade", "the Frost Maw" as a proper noun). These become *sensation and event* in the
  prose, and *annotation* in a footnote.
  - **One exception — "the Shadow."** As Tolkien's cosmic evil (Sauron's Shadow, "the Shadow
    spreads," "servants of the Shadow"), it is in-world and belongs in the prose. Only the
    *mechanic* is banished: never "she gained a point of Shadow," "her Shadow score," "a
    Shadow-scar" as a stat. Render the mechanical Shadow-gain as dread/cold/weight in the
    prose, and note the point(s) in the footnote.
- **Convention (Markdown):** place a reference at the moment of the event and define it at the
  foot of the chapter:

  > She loosed, and the shaft took the warg through the shoulder.[^r1]
  >
  > `[^r1]:` **Bow** vs. the warg — 2 successes; a Piercing Blow. Warg driven off.

- **What earns a footnote:** consequential rolls (combat, stakes-bearing skill tests),
  **Shadow** gains and permanent **Scars**, **Hope** expenditures, Fellowship-phase and
  advancement beats, and Journey events. *Not* every trivial roll.
- **Footnote voice:** terse, mechanical, in TOR 2e vocabulary — deliberately the opposite of
  the literary prose. Optional table/GM colour is welcome ("GM: escalated the ambush after a
  raised Eye").
- **Rendering:** the site will show these as superscript references linking to a per-chapter
  **Ledger** at the chapter's end. *(Requires a small `build_site.py` addition — footnote
  collection + render — to be added when we begin the rewrite.)*

## 8. Formatting Conventions

- **Scene break:** a centred `⁂` on its own line (renders as ❦). A scene break is also where a
  POV anchor may change (§1).
- **Dialogue:** one speaker per paragraph; a new paragraph on every change of speaker. Curly
  quotation marks; closing quotes never dropped.
- **Dateline:** each chapter opens with an italic dateline — `*Víressë 4, 2966*` — in a single
  consistent format. Every chapter is dated.
- **Thoughts:** *italics.*
- **Chapter heading:** `# Chapter N: Title`.
- **Never in the prose:** game terms (→ §5 footnotes), stray/emphasis asterisks, leaked
  section headers, underscore rules, present-tense session notes.

## 9. The Rewrite Workflow

The bible is the spec; the work is a **chapter-by-chapter rewrite**, book by book, in order.

Per chapter:
1. Set the **POV anchor** and **dateline**; confirm against the calendar/age ladder.
2. **Rewrite to the Shannon register** (§3) — scene over summary, show over tell, tics retired.
3. **Lift mechanics into footnotes** (§5).
4. Apply the **glossary** (§6) and check every fact against the **ledger** (§7).
5. Render **intimacy** at its intended level with full craft (§4).
6. Log the chapter as done; add any new canon to the ledger.

**Recommended first move:** pilot **one chapter** end-to-end to calibrate the target voice
before scaling — a romance-forward chapter (to fix the intimacy register) and/or Book 1 Ch1
(the opener that sets first impressions). Compare against the source; lock the voice; then run
the books in sequence.
